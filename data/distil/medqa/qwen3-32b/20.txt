### Key Diagnostic Considerations

1. **Wernicke's Encephalopathy**:
   - Classic triad: **Ophthalmoplegia**, **ataxia**, and **confusion**.
   - Caused by **thiamine (vitamin B1) deficiency**, which is common in chronic alcoholics due to poor nutrition and impaired absorption.
   - Thiamine is essential for **glucose metabolism**, especially in the brain.

2. **Hypoglycemia**:
   - Serum glucose of 47 mg/dL is **critically low**.
   - In alcoholics, this can result from **hepatic dysfunction**, **inadequate caloric intake**, and **increased insulin sensitivity**.
   - **Dextrose administration** is required, but must be done **with caution** in the context of suspected thiamine deficiency.

3. **Scurvy (Vitamin C Deficiency)**:
   - The **perifollicular hemorrhages**, **bleeding gums**, and **easy bruising** are consistent with **scurvy**.
   - However, the **acute neurological presentation** is more suggestive of **Wernicke's encephalopathy**, which is a **medical emergency**.

The clinical scenario describes a 45-year-old man with a **chronic productive cough** and a **25-year smoking history**, which are classic risk factors for **chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)**—specifically **chronic bronchitis**. The **enlarged heart** on chest radiograph is a sign of **cor pulmonale**, which is right-sided heart enlargement due to **pulmonary hypertension**, a common complication of advanced COPD.

---

### Pulmonary Function Test (PFT) Interpretation

PFTs are essential in differentiating lung diseases. In this case, the patient likely has **airway obstruction** due to chronic bronchitis or emphysema. The PFT pattern in obstructive lung disease is characterized by:

- **Reduced FEV1 (Forced Expiratory Volume in 1 second)**
- **Reduced FVC (Forced Vital Capacity)**
- **Normal or decreased FEV1/FVC ratio** (typically < 0.70)

This is in contrast to **restrictive lung disease**, where total lung capacity is decreased, but FEV1/FVC is **normal or increased**.

---

### Evaluating the Answer Choices

Let’s assess each option in the context of expected PFT patterns:

- **A: Asymptomatic asthma**  
  Asthma is an obstructive disease but is usually **reversible**, and PFTs often show **variable airflow obstruction**. This is less likely in a long-standing, chronic, and smoking-related cough.

- **B: Silicosis**  
  Silicosis is an **interstitial lung disease** (ILD) caused by inhalation of silica dust. It presents with a **restrictive PFT pattern**, not obstructive. Not a match.

- **C: Emphysema** ✅  
  Emphysema is a form of COPD with **obstructive PFT findings**—low FEV1, low FVC, and reduced FEV1/FVC ratio. This is the **most likely PFT pattern** in our patient, who has a chronic productive cough and a long smoking history. The enlarged heart is consistent with **cor pulmonale** secondary to **COPD**.

- **D: Coal worker's pneumonitis**  
  Also an ILD, this is similar to silicosis and presents with **restrictive PFTs**. Not consistent with the obstructive pattern expected in this case.

- **E: Prior lung radiation**  
  Radiation pneumonitis or fibrosis can cause **restrictive lung disease**. Again, this is not consistent with the clinical presentation and expected PFT findings here.

---

### Conclusion

The clinical features and PFT pattern most closely resemble those of **emphysema**, which is an obstructive lung disease and a common manifestation of long-term cigarette smoking.

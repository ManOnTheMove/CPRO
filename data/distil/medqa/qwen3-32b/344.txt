### Cardiac Action Potential Phases and Ion Channels

The cardiac action potential in ventricular myocytes consists of five phases:

- **Phase 0 (Rapid Depolarization):** Mediated by **fast sodium channels**.
- **Phase 1 (Initial Repolarization):** Brief repolarization due to transient outward K⁺ current.
- **Phase 2 (Plateau):** Characterized by a balance of **inward Ca²⁺ current** and **outward K⁺ current**.
- **Phase 3 (Repolarization):** Outward K⁺ current dominates.
- **Phase 4 (Resting Membrane Potential):** Maintained by **inward rectifier K⁺ channels (IK1)** and other background currents.

---

### Focus on the Plateau Phase (Phase 2)

The **plateau phase** is crucial for maintaining the prolonged duration of the cardiac action potential. It is primarily sustained by **inward calcium current** through **L-type voltage-gated calcium channels**. These channels open in response to depolarization and allow Ca²⁺ influx, which balances the outward K⁺ current.

If these channels are dysfunctional, the balance is disrupted, leading to altered action potential duration and potentially triggering arrhythmias such as **ventricular tachycardia or re-entrant arrhythmias**.

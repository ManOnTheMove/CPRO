### Clinical Context

The patient presents with **mitral regurgitation (MR)**, as suggested by the **holosystolic murmur at the apex** that radiates to the axilla and the **early diastolic sound (S3)**, consistent with volume overload. Mitral regurgitation is characterized by **retrograde blood flow** from the left ventricle (LV) to the left atrium (LA) during systole.

### Understanding Retrograde Flow in Mitral Regurgitation

The **volume of retrograde flow** (i.e., the amount of blood that leaks back into the left atrium during systole) is influenced by several factors, including:

- **Pressure gradient** between the left ventricle and left atrium
- **Effective regurgitant orifice area**
- **Duration of systole**
- **Afterload (systemic vascular resistance)**

The **pressure gradient** is the most important determinant of the volume of blood regurgitating. It is the difference between the **left ventricular systolic pressure** and the **left atrial pressure**.

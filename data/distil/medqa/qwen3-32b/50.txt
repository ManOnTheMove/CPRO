### Clinical Presentation

- **Progressive headache, blurry vision, and nausea** – classic signs of **increased intracranial pressure (ICP)**.
- **Lethargy and fever (39.1°C)** – indicating an active infection or inflammatory process.
- **Bilateral optic disc swelling (papilledema)** – a strong indicator of raised ICP.
- **Absent corneal reflex in the right eye and reduced facial sensation** – suggesting **cranial nerve (CN) V and VII dysfunction**, particularly on the right side.
- **Elevated fibrin degradation products (FDPs)** – a marker of **coagulation and fibrinolysis**, consistent with clot formation and breakdown.

### Differential Diagnosis

**A. Cerebral Venous Thrombosis (CVT)**  
- **Strongly supported by the clinical context**: CVT is a well-known complication of **middle ear infections** (otitis media) that can spread to the **cavernous sinus** or other cerebral veins.
- **Increased ICP** is a hallmark of CVT, explaining the **papilledema, headache, and visual disturbances**.
- **Cranial nerve palsies**, especially of **CN V and VII**, are common in **cavernous sinus thrombosis**, a subtype of CVT.
- **Fibrin degradation products** are elevated due to **thrombus formation and subsequent fibrinolysis**.
- **Recent infection** is a known risk factor for CVT.

**B. Hypertensive Emergency**  
- While her blood pressure is elevated, it is not extremely high to suggest a hypertensive crisis.
- The **absence of acute encephalopathy** and **lack of retinal hemorrhages** or **papilledema not typically seen in hypertensive encephalopathy** make this less likely.
- No evidence of **acute target-organ damage** (e.g., renal failure, ECG changes).

**C. Subarachnoid Hemorrhage (SAH)**  
- SAH typically presents with a **"thunderclap" headache**, **stiff neck**, and **meningeal signs**, which are **not present here**.
- No evidence of **blood in the CSF** or **aneurysmal history** supports this.
- **Fibrin degradation products** are not a typical feature of SAH.

**D. Idiopathic Intracranial Hypertension (IIH)**  
- IIH can cause **papilledema and headache**, especially in **obese women of reproductive age**, but this patient has **no mention of obesity**.
- **No evidence of chronic ICP elevation** or **visual field loss**.
- **No infection or coagulopathy**, and **FDPs are not elevated** in IIH.

**E. Viral Meningitis**  
- Viral meningitis is associated with **fever, headache, and neck stiffness**, but **papilledema** is **not a typical finding**.
- **Cranial nerve palsies** are rare in viral meningitis.
- **Fibrin degradation products** are not elevated in this condition.

The most consistent diagnosis with the **clinical findings, imaging implications, and laboratory results** is **Cerebral Venous Thrombosis (CVT)**.

### Pathophysiology of Silicosis

Silicosis is an **interstitial lung disease** caused by the **inhalation and deposition of fine silica dust** in the lungs. The **primary mechanism** involves the **phagocytosis of silica particles by alveolar macrophages**. However, unlike other inhaled particles that can be safely processed and removed, **silica is highly toxic** to these macrophages.

When macrophages attempt to engulf silica particles, they become **activated and damaged**, leading to the release of **pro-inflammatory cytokines** and **oxidative stress mediators**. This cascade results in **fibroblast activation and collagen deposition**, ultimately leading to **pulmonary fibrosis**.

This **fibrotic process** reduces lung compliance and impairs gas exchange, which are hallmark features of **restrictive lung disease**—exactly what is described in the PFT findings.

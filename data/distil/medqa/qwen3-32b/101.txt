### Clinical Scenario Analysis

The patient is a 31-year-old woman who recently returned from a cruise and has traveled in Central and South America. She is experiencing **bloody diarrhea**, **nausea**, **right upper quadrant abdominal pain**, and **liver abscesses**. Stool examination reveals **blood and neutrophils**, indicating an **inflammatory process** in the gastrointestinal tract.

---

### Key Diagnostic Clues

- **Travel history** to regions where **parasitic and bacterial enteric infections** are common.
- **Bloody diarrhea** with **neutrophils** in the stool suggests **invasive colitis**.
- **Right upper quadrant pain** and **liver abscesses** are characteristic of **amoebic liver abscess**, which is a complication of **Entamoeba histolytica** infection.
- The presence of **blood and neutrophils** in the stool further supports an **invasive enteric pathogen**.

---

### Pathophysiology and Organism Behavior

**Entamoeba histolytica** is a **protozoan parasite** that causes **amoebic dysentery**. It invades the intestinal mucosa and leads to the formation of **characteristic flask-shaped ulcers** in the colon. These ulcers are **narrow at the entrance and widen at the base**, resembling a flask. The ulcers are associated with **inflammation and necrosis**, which explains the presence of **blood and neutrophils** in the stool.

The parasite can also disseminate hematogenously and cause **liver abscesses**, especially in the **right lobe of the liver**, which aligns with the patient's **right upper quadrant pain**.

- **C: Non-invasive trophozoites** – This is characteristic of **Giardia**, which causes **non-bloody, non-invasive diarrhea**, not the presentation seen here.

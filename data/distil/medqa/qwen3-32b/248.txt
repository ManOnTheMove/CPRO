### Genetic Mechanism Involved

In hereditary cancer syndromes like Li-Fraumeni syndrome, individuals are born with one **mutated copy** of a tumor suppressor gene (in this case, **TP53**). Tumor suppressor genes function to prevent uncontrolled cell growth. When the second (normal) copy of the gene is lost—through a mutation or chromosomal rearrangement—this is referred to as **loss of heterozygosity (LOH)**. This "two-hit" model is essential for tumor development in these syndromes.

### Clinical Scenario Overview

This 25-year-old woman presents with **dyspnea and mild chest pain on exertion**, with a **history of similar symptoms** and **abnormal arterial blood gas (ABG)** findings from the previous year. The ABG showed **respiratory alkalosis** (pH 7.51, pCO₂ 32 mmHg) and **mild hypoxemia** (pO₂ 77 mmHg). These findings are consistent with **chronic hyperventilation**, possibly due to **elevated pulmonary vascular resistance**.

The **ECG findings** (right axis deviation and right heart strain) and **chest X-ray** (enlarged pulmonary arteries) further support **pulmonary hypertension** or **right ventricular hypertrophy**. The **lung perfusion scan** has already ruled out **acute pulmonary embolism** as a cause of her symptoms. There are **no parenchymal infiltrates**, which makes interstitial lung disease or pneumonia less likely.

---

### Differential Diagnoses to Consider

1. **Pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH)** – especially if idiopathic or associated with connective tissue diseases.
2. **Chronic thromboembolic pulmonary hypertension (CTEPH)** – though the perfusion scan is low probability, it's not completely ruled out.
3. **Congenital heart disease** – especially right-to-left shunts or Eisenmenger syndrome.
4. **Eisenmenger syndrome** – a possible cause in young patients with cyanotic heart disease.
5. **Other forms of pulmonary hypertension** – such as those due to left heart disease or lung disease.

---

### Diagnostic Approach

Given the **clinical and imaging findings**, the **next logical diagnostic step** is to **evaluate the heart's structure and function**, particularly the **right side**, and **estimate pulmonary artery pressures**. This is best achieved with:

#### **B: Echocardiography**

Echocardiography is a **first-line, non-invasive test** in suspected pulmonary hypertension. It can:

- Assess **right ventricular size and function**.
- Estimate **pulmonary artery systolic pressure**.
- Detect signs of **right heart strain**.
- Rule out **structural heart disease**.

It is also **cost-effective**, **readily available**, and **safe**, especially in a young patient without clear evidence of acute disease.

### Why Not the Other Options?

- **A: PET scan of the heart** – Not typically used in the workup of pulmonary hypertension or right heart strain. More for assessing myocardial metabolism or tumors.

- **D: Lung biopsy / E: Transbronchial biopsy** – Invasive and not indicated in the absence of interstitial lung disease or a clear infiltrative pattern on imaging.

### Understanding Antihistamine Generations

Antihistamines are broadly categorized into two generations:

- **First-generation antihistamines** (e.g., chlorpheniramine, diphenhydramine, promethazine, hydroxyzine) are known to cross the blood-brain barrier, leading to sedation, drowsiness, and impaired cognitive function. These are often associated with significant side effects that make them unsuitable for individuals who need to remain alert, such as drivers.

- **Second-generation antihistamines** (e.g., cetirizine, loratadine, fexofenadine) are designed to be more selective and less likely to cross the blood-brain barrier. As a result, they cause fewer sedative effects and are preferred in patients who need to remain alert.

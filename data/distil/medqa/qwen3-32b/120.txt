### Neuroimaging in Schizophrenia

Structural MRI findings in schizophrenia are well-documented and include:

- **Enlarged cerebral ventricles**, particularly the **lateral and third ventricles**.
- **Decreased cortical and subcortical gray matter volume**, especially in the **prefrontal and temporal lobes**.
- **Reduced hippocampal and amygdalar volume**.
- **White matter abnormalities**, though typically not the prominent finding in this case.

These structural changes are not specific to any one subtype of schizophrenia but are **commonly seen in chronic cases**, especially when symptoms have been present for years, as in this patient.

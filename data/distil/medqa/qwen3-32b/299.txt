The **first-line treatment** for RA is **methotrexate**, a **disease-modifying antirheumatic drug (DMARD)**. It is preferred due to its **efficacy**, **cost**, and **favorable side effect profile** compared to other DMARDs or biologics.

### Side Effect Profile of Methotrexate

Methotrexate has several known side effects:

- **Hepatotoxicity** (liver damage)
- **Myelosuppression** (low blood cell counts)
- **Gastrointestinal upset** (nausea, vomiting)
- **Pneumonitis** (inflammation of the lung tissue)
- **Increased risk of infections**, including **reactivation of latent tuberculosis**, though this is more commonly associated with **biologic DMARDs** such as **TNF inhibitors**.

Among these, **pneumonitis** is a **well-documented and potentially serious side effect of methotrexate**, occurring in approximately 1–3% of patients. It is typically **dose-dependent** and can present with **dyspnea, cough, and hypoxia**, and requires immediate discontinuation of the drug.

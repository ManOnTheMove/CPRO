### Clinical Context

The patient is a 4-year-old girl presenting with **recurrent colicky abdominal pain**, **hematuria**, and **hexagonal-shaped crystals** in the urine. A **positive sodium cyanide nitroprusside test** is also noted.

---

### Key Diagnostic Clues

1. **Hexagonal-shaped crystals** in the urine are characteristic of **cystine crystals**, which are a hallmark of **cystinuria**.
2. **Positive sodium cyanide nitroprusside test** confirms the presence of **cystine** in the urine.
3. **Recurrent abdominal pain and hematuria** are consistent with **urinary stone formation**, a common complication of cystinuria.

---

### Understanding Cystinuria

Cystinuria is an **autosomal recessive** disorder caused by mutations in **SLC3A1 or SLC7A9** genes. These genes encode a **dipeptide transporter** (rBAT/b0,+AT) that is responsible for the **reabsorption of cystine, ornithine, lysine, and arginine** in the **proximal renal tubules and intestinal epithelium**.

- In **the kidneys**, the defect leads to **excessive urinary excretion of these amino acids**, especially **cystine**, which is poorly soluble and tends to form **hexagonal crystals** and **stones**.
- In **the intestines**, the same transporter defect causes **impaired reabsorption of these amino acids**, but this typically has minimal clinical impact due to the large dietary intake.

---

### Evaluating the Options

- **A: Increased stool fat content**  
  This is more indicative of **malabsorption syndromes** (e.g., celiac disease, pancreatic insufficiency), not related to cystinuria.

- **B: Inability of intercalated cells to secrete H+**  
  This is a feature of **distal renal tubular acidosis**, not cystinuria.

- **C: Increased urine homocysteine levels**  
  This is associated with **homocystinuria**, a different inborn error of metabolism involving **methionine metabolism**, not amino acid transport.

- **D: Impaired intestinal ornithine reabsorption** ✅  
  This is a **direct consequence of the defective transporter** in cystinuria. While the **renal defect is the clinically significant one**, the **intestine also expresses the same transporter**, so **impaired reabsorption of ornithine** in the intestines would be expected in further evaluation.

- **E: Urease-positive bacteria in the renal pelvis**  
  This is relevant to **struvite (triple phosphate) stones**, which are associated with **urinary tract infections** by organisms like *Proteus*, but not cystine stones.

### Understanding Brain Death

Brain death is a clinical and legal definition of death. It is the irreversible cessation of all functions of the entire brain, including the brainstem. Once brain death is confirmed through a series of rigorous neurological exams (typically by two independent physicians), the individual is considered legally dead, even if the heart continues to beat due to mechanical ventilation. The heart may continue to function as long as the ventilator is in place, but this does not indicate that the person is alive.

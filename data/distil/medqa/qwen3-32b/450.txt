### Understanding the Antiviral in Question

**Ribavirin** is a broad-spectrum antiviral drug that inhibits viral RNA synthesis. It is used in the treatment of:

- **RSV (Respiratory Syncytial Virus)**: Especially in **neonates and immunocompromised patients**.
- **Hepatitis C virus (HCV)**: Ribavirin is **often used in combination with direct-acting antivirals (DAAs)** or **interferon-based regimens** for the treatment of **chronic hepatitis C**.

While ribavirin **can be part of a combination therapy**, it is **not used** in the treatment of:

- **HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus)** – Treated with **antiretroviral therapy (ART)**, which includes drugs like **tenofovir, emtricitabine, and integrase inhibitors**.
- **Hepatitis B virus (HBV)** – Treated with **nucleos(t)ide analogs** such as **tenofovir or entecavir**.
- **HSV (Herpes Simplex Virus)** – Treated with **acyclovir, valacyclovir**, or **famciclovir**.
- **Rhinovirus** – **No specific antiviral** is used; management is supportive.

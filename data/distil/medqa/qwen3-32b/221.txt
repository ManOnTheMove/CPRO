### Key Considerations

1. **H. pylori Treatment and Follow-Up**:
   - The standard treatment for H. pylori includes **triple or quadruple therapy**, which typically lasts **10–14 days**.
   - After completing treatment, it is **crucial to confirm eradication** to prevent recurrence of ulcers or complications like gastric cancer.
   - **Non-invasive tests** are preferred for follow-up:
     - **Urea breath test (UBT)** – highly sensitive and specific.
     - **Stool antigen test** – also non-invasive.
   - **Endoscopy with biopsy** is more invasive and typically reserved for patients with alarm symptoms (e.g., weight loss, anemia, bleeding), or if non-invasive tests are inconclusive.

2. **Timing of Post-Treatment Testing**:
   - It is generally recommended to wait **at least 4 weeks** after completing therapy to perform H. pylori testing.
   - However, the **urea breath test can be performed as early as 2 weeks** after finishing treatment if there is no ongoing PPI use, or if the PPI has been withheld for 2 weeks before the test.
   - In this case, the patient is on **omeprazole**, a PPI, for the full 2 weeks. While PPIs can reduce gastric acid and potentially interfere with urea breath test results, **some guidelines accept testing as early as 2 weeks**, especially when the test is performed **after** stopping the PPI.

To evaluate which statement about **Positive Predictive Value (PPV)** is correct, we begin by understanding its definition and how it is influenced by **disease prevalence**.

---

### Understanding PPV

The **Positive Predictive Value (PPV)** is defined as:

$$
\text{PPV} = \frac{\text{True Positives}}{\text{True Positives + False Positives}}
$$

This represents the **probability that a person with a positive test result actually has the disease**. It is a **post-test probability**, meaning it depends on the **prevalence of the disease in the population** being tested.

---

### Impact of Disease Prevalence

- **Low Prevalence**: Fewer people have the disease. Even with a highly specific test, the number of **false positives** (healthy individuals incorrectly labeled as positive) can become relatively large compared to **true positives**. This **lowers the PPV**.

- **High Prevalence**: More people have the disease. The number of **true positives** increases relative to **false positives**, which **raises the PPV**.

Thus, **PPV is directly influenced by disease prevalence**.

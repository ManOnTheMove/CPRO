### Understanding the Pathophysiology

**Fitz-Hugh-Curtis syndrome** is characterized by **perihepatic adhesions** (also known as "violin-string" adhesions), resulting from the **spread of pelvic inflammation** via the **falciform ligament**. This syndrome is typically associated with **PID**, which is most commonly caused by **Neisseria gonorrhoeae** and **Chlamydia trachomatis**. 

Although **Trichomonas vaginalis** can also cause PID, it is **less commonly associated** with Fitz-Hugh-Curtis syndrome. The **acute inflammation** described in this case, along with the **perihepatic adhesions**, supports an infectious etiology consistent with **gonococcal PID**.

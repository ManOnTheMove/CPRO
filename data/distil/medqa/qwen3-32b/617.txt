The child exhibits signs of **respiratory distress** (stridor, tachypnea), **facial edema**, **rash**, **hypotension**, and **tachycardia**, all of which are hallmark features of systemic anaphylaxis.

---

### Key Clinical Features Supporting Anaphylaxis:
- **Acute onset** after exposure to a known allergen (seafood).
- **Respiratory symptoms** (cough, shortness of breath, stridor), suggesting **airway compromise**.
- **Facial edema** and **rash** (urticaria) are classic signs of allergic reaction.
- **Hypotension (80/52 mm Hg)** and **tachycardia (122 bpm)** indicate **systemic vasodilation and shock**.

---

### Immediate Management of Anaphylaxis:
The **first-line treatment** for anaphylaxis is **epinephrine**, which is the **only medication that can effectively reverse the life-threatening components** of the reaction, including airway edema, bronchospasm, and hypotension.

- **Intramuscular (IM) epinephrine** is the **preferred route** in both prehospital and emergency department settings. It is **rapidly absorbed**, **safe**, and **effective**, particularly in **pediatric patients**.
- **Intravenous (IV) epinephrine** is reserved for **severe cases** where IM is not sufficient or when **IV access is available** and **hemodynamic instability persists**. However, **IM is typically adequate and faster to administer** in the acute setting.

---

### Why the Other Options Are Not Appropriate:
- **Oral diphenhydramine (B)** and **extra-strength topical diphenhydramine (D)** are **antihistamines**. While they may alleviate **mild cutaneous symptoms**, they are **not effective** in reversing **airway or cardiovascular compromise**.
- **Inhaled sodium cromolyn (E)** is a **mast cell stabilizer**, used **prophylactically** in asthma or allergic rhinitis, but it has **no role in acute anaphylaxis**.

---

### Conclusion:
Given the **life-threatening nature of the presentation**, the **most appropriate next step** is to administer **intramuscular epinephrine** to rapidly counteract the allergic cascade and prevent further deterioration.

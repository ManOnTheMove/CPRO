### Key Diagnostic Clues

- **ECG findings**: Prolonged PR interval and narrow QRS complex.
- **Lack of response to atropine**.
- **Cold, clammy extremities** and **hypotension**.
- **Wheezing and rhonchi** may suggest bronchospasm or secretions, but this is not the primary concern here.
- **No pinpoint pupils**, ruling out opioid overdose.

---

### Differential Diagnosis

Given the ECG findings and lack of atropine response, the most likely drug classes involved are:

#### 1. **Beta-blockers**
- Common causes of **bradycardia**, **hypotension**, and **prolonged PR interval**.
- **Atropine is often ineffective** in beta-blocker overdose due to beta-receptor blockade.
- **Glucagon** is the preferred treatment in this setting, as it acts via the **cAMP pathway** to increase heart rate and contractility, bypassing beta-adrenergic receptors.

#### 2. **Calcium Channel Blockers (CCBs)**
- May also cause bradycardia, hypotension, and prolonged PR interval.
- However, CCB overdose typically causes **wider QRS** or **AV block** and is managed with **calcium**, **insulin**, and **sodium bicarbonate** (in cases of severe acidosis or widened QRS).
- The QRS in this case is **narrow**, making CCB less likely than beta-blocker.

#### 3. **Tricyclic Antidepressants (TCAs)**
- TCAs cause **wide QRS** and **ventricular dysrhythmias**, which are not seen here.
- **Sodium bicarbonate** is the treatment of choice for TCA overdose.

#### 4. **Opioids**
- Would present with **respiratory depression**, **pinpoint pupils**, and possibly **bradycardia**, but not typically with **prolonged PR interval** or **narrow QRS**.
- **Naloxone** is used for opioid overdose, but is unlikely to be effective here.

---

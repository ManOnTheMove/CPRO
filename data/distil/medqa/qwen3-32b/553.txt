### Understanding *Yersinia enterocolitica* Transmission

*Yersinia enterocolitica* is a zoonotic pathogen primarily transmitted via the **fecal-oral route**. Common sources include:

- **Contaminated food**, especially **undercooked pork** (a major source)
- **Contaminated water**
- **Contact with infected animals**, especially **pigs**, **rodents**, or **livestock**, which are natural reservoirs for the bacteria

Transmission through **petting zoos** is well-documented, particularly when children have close contact with animals and then fail to **wash their hands** before eating or touching their face. These environments can easily become contaminated with *Yersinia*, *Salmonella*, or *E. coli* if hygiene is not properly maintained.

To determine which action represents *sublimation*, it's essential to understand what sublimation means in the context of psychological defense mechanisms.

---

### Understanding Sublimation

**Sublimation** is a mature defense mechanism in which an individual redirects unacceptable or negative emotions into socially acceptable or constructive activities. For example, a person who feels anger might channel that energy into sports, art, or work. It is a positive and adaptive way of handling emotions, transforming them into productive outlets rather than destructive or harmful behaviors.

---

### Evaluating the Options

- **A: Channeling his anger into training for his track meet**  
  This is a textbook example of sublimation. The boy is redirecting his emotional energy (anger and frustration from the betrayal) into a constructive activity (training for a track meet). This not only helps him manage his emotions but also contributes to a positive goal.

- **B: Instigating a physical fight with his girlfriend’s other partner**  
  This is an expression of raw aggression without any redirection or constructive purpose. It is not sublimation, and it likely reflects acting out or impulsive behavior.

- **C: Making jokes about the situation and incorporating them into his comedy routine**  
  This reflects the use of **humor** as a defense mechanism, not sublimation. Humor can be a way to distance oneself from pain, but it is not the same as redirecting negative impulses into constructive behavior.

- **D: Not thinking about the upsetting situation now or in the future**  
  This is an example of **denial** or **avoidance**, where the individual refuses to acknowledge the problem. It is a less mature defense mechanism and not sublimation.

- **E: Not thinking about the situation until after the track meet, then confronting and processing his feelings**  
  This is more in line with **suppression** or **delayed emotional processing**. It is a temporary coping strategy but not sublimation, which involves active redirection of emotions into productive activities.

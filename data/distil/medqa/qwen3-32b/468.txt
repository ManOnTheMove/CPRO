### **Overview of Bone Healing Process**

Bone healing is a complex, multi-stage process that involves the following phases:

1. **Inflammatory Phase**: Immediately after injury, inflammatory cells such as neutrophils and macrophages migrate to the site to clear debris and initiate the healing process.
2. **Repair Phase**: Involves the formation of a soft callus (fibrocartilage) followed by a hard callus (woven bone).
3. **Remodeling Phase**: The woven bone is replaced by lamellar bone, restoring the original structure and strength of the bone.

Throughout this process, cytokines and growth factors play critical roles in cell recruitment, differentiation, and matrix deposition.

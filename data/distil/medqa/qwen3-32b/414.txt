The clinical scenario describes a 6-year-old boy with dark urine that has been present since infancy and is not associated with any significant medical symptoms or signs. The key physical findings are the absence of abnormalities on examination and normal vital signs, suggesting a benign condition. The presence of dark staining in the toilet is a classic clue for a metabolic disorder involving the excretion of a substance that oxidizes upon exposure to air.

---

**Dark urine with oxidation to a dark color** is a hallmark of **alkaptonuria**, a rare autosomal recessive disorder caused by a deficiency in the enzyme **homogentisate 1,2-dioxygenase**. This enzyme is responsible for the conversion of **homogentisic acid** into **maleylacetoacetate** in the tyrosine degradation pathway. When the enzyme is deficient, homogentisic acid accumulates and is excreted in the urine, where it oxidizes and polymerizes upon exposure to air, resulting in the characteristic dark staining.

---

**Tyrosine metabolism pathway:**

1. **Tyrosine** → **Homogentisic acid** (via tyrosine hydroxylase and transaminase)
2. **Homogentisic acid** → **Maleylacetoacetate** (via **homogentisate 1,2-dioxygenase**)
3. **Maleylacetoacetate** → Further metabolized into fumarate and acetoacetate

In **alkaptonuria**, the enzyme **homogentisate 1,2-dioxygenase** is defective, leading to **impaired formation of maleylacetoacetate**. Therefore, the correct answer is the compound that is not formed due to this enzymatic block.

1. **Infection Risk**:

2. **Wound Closure**:
   - Immediate primary closure is generally **not recommended** for dog bites due to the high risk of **abscess formation**.
   - **Delayed primary closure** or **open management** is preferred unless the wound is superficial and clean.

3. **Rabies Risk and Management**:
   - Rabies is a **potentially fatal** viral infection.
   - For **domestic animals (e.g., dogs)** that are **available for observation**, the standard protocol is **quarantine and 10-day observation**.
   - If the animal is **unavailable or shows signs of rabies**, **post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP)** with **rabies vaccine and rabies immunoglobulin** is required.

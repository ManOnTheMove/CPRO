### DSM-5 Diagnostic Criteria for PTSD

To meet diagnostic criteria for PTSD, the following must be present:

1. **Exposure to actual or threatened death, serious injury, or sexual violence**.
2. **Intrusion symptoms** (e.g., nightmares, flashbacks).
3. **Avoidance** of reminders of the trauma.
4. **Negative alterations in mood and cognition**.
5. **Alterations in arousal and reactivity** (e.g., irritability).
6. **Duration of symptoms is at least one month**.
7. **Clinically significant distress or impairment in functioning**.
8. **Symptoms are not attributable to substance use or another medical condition**.

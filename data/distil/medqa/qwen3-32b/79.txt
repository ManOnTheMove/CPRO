### Clinical Scenario Overview

A 27-year-old woman presents with episodes of syncope, most recently while shoveling snow and previously while running. These events occurred after exertion and were preceded by lightheadedness and shortness of breath. She has a family history of sudden cardiac death at a young age. On physical examination, she has a **systolic ejection murmur** best heard at the **left sternal border**, which **decreases with hand grip**. These clinical findings are classic for **hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM)**.

---

### Pathophysiology and Physical Findings

- **HCM** is characterized by **asymmetric left ventricular hypertrophy** without an identifiable cause (e.g., hypertension or aortic stenosis).
- The hallmark murmur is a **systolic ejection murmur** that **increases with maneuvers that reduce preload** (e.g., Valsalva) and **decreases with maneuvers that increase afterload** (e.g., hand grip). This is due to the **dynamic left ventricular outflow tract obstruction**.
- **Syncope during exertion** is a red flag in HCM, as it is associated with a **high risk of sudden cardiac death**, especially in the presence of a **family history of sudden cardiac death**.

---

### Treatment Strategy

The primary goals of treatment in HCM are to:

1. **Reduce the outflow gradient**
2. **Prevent arrhythmias**
3. **Improve symptoms and quality of life**
4. **Reduce the risk of sudden cardiac death**

#### First-line Medical Therapy:
- **Beta-blockers** (e.g., **metoprolol**) are the **first-line treatment** in symptomatic HCM patients. They reduce heart rate and contractility, thereby decreasing the outflow gradient and improving symptoms. They also help prevent tachycardia-induced worsening of obstruction.

#### Alternative Medical Therapy:
- **Non-dihydropyridine calcium channel blockers** (e.g., **verapamil**) are used if beta-blockers are contraindicated or poorly tolerated.

#### Surgical/Interventional Options:
- **Septal myectomy** is reserved for patients with **severe symptoms** who do not respond to medical therapy.
- **Alcohol septal ablation** is a less invasive alternative to surgery.
- **Implantable cardioverter-defibrillators (ICDs)** are considered in high-risk patients (e.g., those with prior cardiac arrest or significant arrhythmias), but not listed as an option here.

#### Inappropriate Options:
- **Amlodipine** is a **dihydropyridine calcium channel blocker** and a **pure vasodilator**. It can **worsen the outflow obstruction** in HCM and is not recommended.
- A **cardiac pacemaker** is not typically used in uncomplicated HCM and is not a first-line intervention.

---

### Final Answer

Given the patient's symptoms, family history, and physical findings, the most appropriate initial treatment is a **beta-blocker**, specifically **metoprolol**, to reduce the outflow gradient and decrease the risk of syncope and sudden cardiac death.

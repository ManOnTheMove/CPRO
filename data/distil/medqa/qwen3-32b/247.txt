Her **physical examination** reveals **proximal muscle weakness**, and **delayed tendon reflex relaxation with mounding of the muscle surface just before relaxation**, which is a classic sign of **myotonia**. ### Differential Diagnosis

The clinical presentation suggests a **muscle disease** with possible **inflammatory or metabolic etiology**. Several conditions could explain these findings:

1. **Hypothyroidism**: 
   - Common cause of **elevated CK**, **proximal muscle weakness**, and **muscle pain**.
   - Also explains **weight gain**, **constipation**, **dry skin**, and **headaches**.
   - Prolonged use of calamine lotion (which contains **zinc**) may contribute to **zinc toxicity**, which is known to **interfere with thyroid function**.

2. **Polymyositis/Dermatomyositis**:
   - Inflammatory myopathies that present with **proximal muscle weakness** and **elevated CK**.
   - Typically associated with **inflammatory markers** and possibly **rash** (not reported here).
   - However, these are **less likely before ruling out hypothyroidism**, a more common and easily treatable condition.

3. **Myotonic Disorders** (e.g., **myotonic dystrophy**):
   - The **mounding of muscle with delayed relaxation of reflexes** is **suggestive of myotonia**.
   - These are **genetic disorders**, and while possible, they are **less common than hypothyroidism** and **do not explain the constellation of metabolic symptoms**.

4. **Lambert-Eaton Myasthenic Syndrome (LEMS)**:
   - Presents with **proximal weakness**, often associated with **cancer**.
   - Weakness typically **improves with repeated use** (not described here).
   - **CK is usually normal**, so **less likely** in this case.

5. **Drug-Induced Myopathy**:
   - Atenolol and amlodipine are **not typically associated with CK elevation**.
   - Calamine lotion (zinc-based) could theoretically contribute to **zinc toxicity**, but **does not directly cause CK elevation** unless in the context of **rhabdomyolysis**, which is **not suggested here**.

6. **Temporal Arteritis**:
   - Presents with **headaches, jaw claudication, and vision changes**.
   - **Unlikely in a 43-year-old**, as it is rare in this age group.

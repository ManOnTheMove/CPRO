### Differential Diagnosis

Let's evaluate the options:

- Characterized by **severe speech impairment**, **ataxia**, and a **happy demeanor** (frequent laughter).
- **Developmental delay is typically present from birth**, not a regression.
- Not the best fit for this case.

#### **B. Rett syndrome**
- **Classic presentation** includes:
  - **Normal development for the first 6–18 months**
  - **Regression** in language and motor skills (loss of purposeful hand use)
  - **Stereotypic hand movements** (e.g., wringing, clapping)
  - **Autistic-like features** and **decerebrate posturing**
  - **X-linked dominant** with **sporadic mutations**, so **no family history** is expected in most cases.
- This **closely matches** the clinical presentation of the patient.

#### **C. Beckwith-Wiedemann syndrome**
- Features include **overgrowth**, **macroglossia**, **omphalocele**, and **neoplasia risk**.
- Not associated with **regression** or **hand wringing**.
- **Not consistent** with this case.

#### **D. Prader-Willi syndrome**
- Presents with **infantile hypotonia**, **feeding difficulties**, and later **hyperphagia** and **obesity**.
- **Developmental delay** is present but not typically **regressive**.
- The child here is **well nourished**, which is inconsistent with early feeding issues seen in this syndrome.

#### **E. McCune-Albright syndrome**
- Features include **precocious puberty**, **bone abnormalities**, and **café-au-lait spots**.
- No **neurodevelopmental regression** or **hand stereotypies** are associated with it.
- **Does not fit** the clinical picture.

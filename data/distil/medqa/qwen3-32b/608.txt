### Role of SNARE Proteins
SNARE (Soluble N-ethylmaleimide-sensitive factor Attachment Protein REceptor) proteins are essential for the exocytosis of synaptic vesicles. They facilitate the fusion of these vesicles with the presynaptic membrane, allowing the release of neurotransmitters into the synaptic cleft. When SNARE proteins are cleaved, neurotransmitter release is blocked.

---

### Role of Renshaw Cells
Renshaw cells are inhibitory interneurons located in the spinal cord. They are activated by the axons of motor neurons and, in turn, inhibit those same motor neurons via autogenic inhibition. This feedback mechanism helps regulate motor neuron firing and prevents excessive or continuous muscle contraction.

---

### Pathophysiological Mechanism of Spider Angiomas in Cirrhosis

Spider angiomas are characterized by a central red spot with radiating capillaries and are commonly seen in patients with chronic liver disease. The mechanism behind their formation is primarily due to **hormonal imbalances**, particularly an **increase in circulating estrogen levels**.

In healthy individuals, the liver metabolizes estrogen. In cirrhosis, the liver's capacity to clear estrogen is impaired, leading to **elevated estrogen levels** in the blood. This estrogen excess causes **vasodilation** of cutaneous blood vessels, especially in the upper body, and contributes to the development of spider angiomas and **palmar erythema** (another common sign in liver disease).

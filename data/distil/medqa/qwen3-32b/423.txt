### Clinical Context

The patient is a **4-year-old boy of Mediterranean descent** presenting with **fever, lethargy, localized swelling and redness in the distal right shin**, and a **history of a blood disorder**. He is **on daily antibiotic prophylaxis** and **received extra vaccinations as an infant**—all of which are highly suggestive of **sickle cell disease**.

Children with **sickle cell disease** are **at increased risk for infections**, especially **Salmonella osteomyelitis**, due to **functional asplenia** and **impaired immune function**. The **daily penicillin prophylaxis**, **extra immunizations**, and **Mediterranean origin** further support this diagnosis.

### Symptom Analysis

- **Fever (102.4°F)** and **lethargy** suggest a **systemic infection**.
- **Swelling and redness** in the **distal shin** with **erythema and edema** are **classic signs of localized infection**.
- **Limping for a week** suggests **chronic or subacute involvement**, possibly **bone-related**.
- **Tachycardia, tachypnea, and hypotension** are concerning for **sepsis**, especially in the context of **febrile illness** and **localized infection**.

### Differential Diagnosis

- **B. Avascular necrosis** – A **chronic complication** of sickle cell disease, usually affecting the **hip or knee**, not the shin. It **does not present with fever or acute infection signs**, so **unlikely**.

- **C. Acute vaso-occlusive episode** – A **pain crisis** in sickle cell disease. While it can cause **pain and swelling**, it **does not typically cause fever or localized erythema**, and the **systemic signs of infection** point more toward **infection than vaso-occlusion**.

- **D. Osteomyelitis** – A **bone infection**, which **matches the clinical picture**:
  - **Fever**
  - **Localized erythema and edema**
  - **Limping**
  - **Systemic signs of infection (lethargy, hypotension)**
  - **High risk in sickle cell patients**, especially for **Salmonella osteomyelitis**
  - **Prophylactic antibiotics may not be sufficient** in all cases, especially if the infection is **resistant or aggressive**

- **E. Thrombophlebitis** – Inflammation of a vein with clot. While it can cause **pain and swelling**, it is **less likely in a 4-year-old** without **clear venous signs**, and **does not explain the fever and systemic symptoms**.

### Understanding Digital Clubbing

Digital clubbing is a physical sign characterized by **softening and bulbous enlargement of the distal phalanges** and **loss of the angle between the nail and the nail bed**. It is a **non-specific** but **classic** finding in several chronic diseases, especially those involving the **lungs, heart, or gastrointestinal system**.

The most common and well-documented cause of clubbing is **chronic hypoxemia**, which is often a result of **pulmonary or cardiac diseases**. The exact pathophysiology is not fully understood, but theories include:
- Vascular proliferation and increased blood flow to the digits
- Hypoxia-induced release of growth factors like vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF)

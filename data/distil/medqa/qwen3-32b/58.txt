### Mechanism of Action of Methylprednisolone

Methylprednisolone is a **glucocorticoid**, which exerts **immunosuppressive and anti-inflammatory effects**. These effects are partly due to:

- **Suppression of lymphocyte function and proliferation**, particularly **T-lymphocytes**.
- **Inhibition of cytokine production** and **leukocyte migration**.
- **Modulation of leukocyte trafficking**, especially **eosinophils and basophils**, which are mobilized from tissues into the circulation and then rapidly cleared or suppressed.

---

### Effects on Blood Cell Counts

Corticosteroids are known to cause **selective leukopenia**, especially in **lymphocytes** and **eosinophils**, and **relative neutrophilia** due to the redistribution of neutrophils from marginated pools into the bloodstream.

To interpret the p-value correctly, we need to recall its formal definition in the context of hypothesis testing.

---

The **p-value** is the probability of obtaining a test statistic as extreme as, or more extreme than, the one observed, **assuming the null hypothesis is true**. In this case, the null hypothesis is that *there is no association between hemoglobin levels and body weight*.

The researcher observed a mean difference in hemoglobin levels of 0.4 g/dL between the normal weight and overweight groups, and the t-test yielded a p-value of 0.10. This means:

- If the null hypothesis were true (i.e., if there were truly no difference in hemoglobin levels between the two BMI groups), there is a **10% chance** of observing a difference of **0.4 g/dL or more** just by random chance.

### Mechanism of Interaction

**Lisinopril** lowers blood pressure by inhibiting the conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II, which reduces vasoconstriction and aldosterone release. It can cause **volume depletion**, especially in the first few days of therapy or when combined with other hypotensive agents.

However, **nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs)** like **ibuprofen** are known to **interact with ACE inhibitors** in a clinically significant way:

- NSAIDs **inhibit prostaglandin synthesis**, which normally helps maintain renal blood flow.
- In patients on ACE inhibitors, especially in a state of **volume depletion** (such as during physical activity or dehydration), **NSAIDs can further reduce renal perfusion**, leading to **acute kidney injury** and **hypotension**.
- This combination is particularly dangerous because **ACE inhibitors already reduce glomerular filtration pressure**, and NSAIDs can **exacerbate this**, especially in a dehydrated state.

### Clinical Context and Question Summary

A 27-year-old woman at **17 weeks of gestation** is being evaluated for a clinical trial related to **nausea management with ginger**. The question asks which of the listed findings would **most likely be abnormal** during pregnancy.

---

### Physiological Changes During Pregnancy

Pregnancy induces a wide range of physiological changes, many of which are **normal adaptations** to support the growing fetus and prepare for delivery. These include:

- **Increased cardiac output** (due to increased blood volume)
- **Mild anemia** (due to plasma volume expansion outpacing red blood cell production)
- **Respiratory alkalosis** (from progesterone-induced hyperventilation)
- **Hypercoagulable state** (elevated fibrinogen and other clotting factors)

---

### Evaluation of Each Option

#### **A. Hypertension**
- **Abnormal** in the context of pregnancy unless the woman has pre-existing (chronic) hypertension.
- **New-onset hypertension** in pregnancy, especially **before 20 weeks**, is **not a normal finding** and may suggest **chronic hypertension or early-onset preeclampsia**.
- **Correct** as the **most likely abnormal** finding.

#### **B. Increased cardiac output**
- **Normal** during pregnancy.
- Cardiac output increases by **30–50%** by mid-pregnancy to meet the demands of the placenta and fetus.

#### **C. Increase in factor VII**
- **Factor VII** levels **do not increase** during pregnancy.
- In fact, **factor VII activity may decrease** slightly, while other clotting factors (e.g., fibrinogen, factors II, V, VIII, X) are elevated.
- An **increase in factor VII** would not be expected and is **not a standard abnormality**.

#### **D. Anemia**
- **Physiologic anemia** is **normal** in pregnancy due to increased plasma volume.
- Hemoglobin levels typically decrease from ~12 g/dL to ~11 g/dL by mid-pregnancy.

#### **E. Respiratory alkalosis**
- **Normal** due to increased tidal volume and minute ventilation driven by progesterone.
- Results in a mild **respiratory alkalosis**, with normal compensatory mechanisms.

---

### Final Determination

Among the listed options, **hypertension** stands out as the **only abnormal finding**, especially in the **absence of a known chronic condition** and at **17 weeks of gestation**.

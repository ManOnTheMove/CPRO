### Clinical Scenario Overview

A 5-year-old boy presents with **abdominal pain**, but no associated **fever, vomiting, diarrhea, or dysuria**. His **last bowel movement was 3 days ago** and was **normal**. On physical examination, he is **restless**, has **mild abdominal distension**, **hyperactive bowel sounds**, and **tachycardia (110 bpm)**. His **ultrasound is unremarkable**, and the remainder of the physical exam is **normal**.

---

### Differential Diagnosis Considerations

Given the child's age and presentation, the differential diagnosis includes:

- **Constipation** (even with a normal recent bowel movement, especially in children, because of possible impaction)
- **Functional abdominal pain** (common in children with no organic cause)
- **Appendicitis** (early stage may be missed on ultrasound)
- **Intussusception** (typically presents with colicky pain, vomiting, and "currant jelly" stools; not seen here)
- **Gastroenteritis** (no vomiting or diarrhea)
- **Urinary tract infection** (no dysuria or fever)
- **Gastrointestinal obstruction** (no vomiting or bilious emesis; ultrasound normal)

---

### Evaluation of the Findings

- **Hyperactive bowel sounds** and **mild distension** may suggest early obstruction or constipation.
- **Tachycardia** and **restlessness** can be due to pain.
- **Normal ultrasound** rules out many acute processes such as appendicitis or intussusception.
- **Absence of systemic symptoms** (fever, vomiting, diarrhea) makes severe infection or obstruction less likely.
- The **normal last bowel movement**, while 3 days ago, does not confirm or exclude constipation, but may suggest a possible impaction.

---

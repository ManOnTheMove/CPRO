### Clinical Reasoning

The most likely diagnosis in this case is **thiamine (vitamin B1) deficiency**, specifically **dry beriberi**, which is characterized by **neurological symptoms** such as peripheral neuropathy, weakness, and atrophy. This is a classic presentation in populations with a diet low in thiamine, such as those subsisting on polished white rice, which lacks the B1-rich bran and germ found in whole grains.

---

### Biochemical Role of Thiamine

Thiamine functions as a **coenzyme in several key metabolic enzymes**, most notably:

- **Pyruvate dehydrogenase**
- **Alpha-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase**
- **Transketolase** (involved in the pentose phosphate pathway)

These enzymes are crucial for energy metabolism, particularly in the **TCA cycle** and **carbohydrate metabolism**. A deficiency in thiamine leads to impaired function of these enzymes, resulting in neurological and metabolic disturbances.

To determine which public health intervention is most likely to decrease the number of mitral valve replacements in adults aged 30–50 in Vietnam, we need to understand the likely underlying cause of this elevated surgical rate.

---

### Etiology and Public Health Context

Mitral valve disease that necessitates surgical replacement is often the result of **rheumatic heart disease (RHD)**. RHD is a long-term complication of **acute rheumatic fever (ARF)**, which itself is caused by an **immune response to untreated or inadequately treated Group A *Streptococcus* (GAS)** infections such as **bacterial pharyngitis** (strep throat).

In low- and middle-income countries like Vietnam, **poor access to antibiotics** for strep throat can lead to higher rates of ARF and subsequent RHD. Over time, this manifests as valvular damage—most commonly affecting the **mitral valve**—which may require surgical intervention in adulthood.

---

### Evaluation of the Options

- **A. Prompt antibiotic treatment for bacterial pharyngitis**  
  This directly addresses the **root cause** of rheumatic heart disease. By treating strep throat early, the chain of events leading to ARF and RHD can be broken. This is a **primary prevention strategy** and is well-supported by public health evidence. This is the **most effective intervention** to reduce the incidence of RHD and, consequently, the need for mitral valve replacement surgery.

- **B. Improved access to drug rehabilitation centers**  
  While drug abuse can have various cardiovascular effects, it is **not a major cause** of mitral valve disease. This intervention is **not directly relevant** to the question.

- **C. Genetic screening program for FBN1 gene mutation**  
  The FBN1 gene is associated with **Marfan syndrome**, which can lead to aortic root dilation and mitral valve prolapse. However, **Marfan syndrome is rare**, and this is a **genetic condition**, not an infectious or public health preventable cause. It is unlikely to be the reason for the increased rates of mitral valve replacement in this age group in Vietnam.

- **D. Childhood vaccination against *Corynebacterium diphtheriae***  
  Diphtheria can cause **myocarditis**, but it is **not a major cause of mitral valve disease**. Moreover, diphtheria is largely **preventable with existing vaccines**, and it is **less common** than rheumatic heart disease in this context.

- **E. Screening echocardiography for prolapsed anterior mitral valve leaflets**  
  Screening for mitral valve prolapse may identify at-risk individuals, but it **does not prevent** the condition or reduce the need for surgery. It is **not a preventive intervention** and is **not likely to reduce the overall rate** of mitral valve replacements.

---

### Conclusion

The most effective public health intervention to reduce the incidence of mitral valve replacement in this population is to **prevent the initial infection that leads to rheumatic heart disease**. Prompt and appropriate antibiotic treatment of **bacterial pharyngitis** is the **most logical and impactful** choice.

### Treatment Considerations

For PMDD, **first-line treatment options** include:

1. **Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs):** These are the most effective and well-supported pharmacologic treatment for PMDD. They can be taken continuously or only during the luteal phase. SSRIs such as **sertraline**, **fluoxetine**, or **paroxetine** are commonly prescribed.
2. **Combined Oral Contraceptives (COCs):** These may help by stabilizing hormonal fluctuations. However, they are not first-line and are typically considered if SSRIs are ineffective or contraindicated.
3. **Serotonin-Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors (SNRIs):** These may be considered if SSRIs are ineffective, but they are not first-line for PMDD.
4. **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT):** A non-pharmacologic first-line option, especially for mild to moderate cases. However, it may take several weeks to show benefit.
5. **Reassurance:** Inappropriate for this patient, as her symptoms are causing significant functional impairment and require active intervention.

### Understanding the Oxygen Parameters

To interpret the expected **arterial blood gas findings**, it is essential to understand the definitions and relationships between the variables:

1. **PaO₂ (Partial Pressure of Oxygen)**: Reflects the pressure of oxygen dissolved in the plasma. It is **not dependent on hemoglobin levels**.
2. **SaO₂ (Oxygen Saturation)**: The percentage of hemoglobin binding sites that are saturated with oxygen. This is also **not directly affected by hemoglobin quantity**.
3. **CaO₂ (Oxygen Content)**: The **total oxygen content** in the blood, which is **directly proportional to hemoglobin concentration**. The formula is:

   $$
   \text{CaO}_2 = (1.34 \times \text{Hb} \times \text{SaO}_2) + (0.003 \times \text{PaO}_2)
   $$

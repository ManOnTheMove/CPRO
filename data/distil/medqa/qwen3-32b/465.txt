### Clinical Context

Sickle cell disease is an **autosomal recessive** disorder caused by a mutation in the **beta-globin gene (HBB)**. The mutation leads to the production of **abnormal hemoglobin (HbS)**, which under certain conditions (like dehydration, hypoxia, or acidosis) causes red blood cells to become **rigid and sickle-shaped**. These deformed cells can block small blood vessels, leading to **vaso-occlusive crises** and the **recurrent pain episodes** described in the patient.

The **pallor and jaundice** are classic signs of **chronic hemolysis**, which is a hallmark of SCD. Dehydration exacerbates the condition by increasing the concentration of sickled cells in the blood, worsening vaso-occlusion and pain.

---

### Genetic Mutation Type

The mutation responsible for sickle cell disease is a **point mutation** in the **HBB gene**, where **adenine is replaced by thymine** at the sixth codon. This substitution results in the **replacement of glutamic acid with valine** in the beta-globin chain. This is a **missense mutation**, as it changes the **amino acid** but does not introduce a stop codon or alter the reading frame.

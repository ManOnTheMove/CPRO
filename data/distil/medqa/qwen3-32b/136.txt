An 18-year-old male with a history of heavy alcohol consumption presents with **unconsciousness**, **severe hypoglycemia**, and **ketoacidemia**. The absence of a diabetes diagnosis implies the hypoglycemia is likely **alcohol-induced**.

### Impact of Elevated [NADH]/[NAD⁺] Ratio

The redox state of the cell, particularly the NADH/NAD⁺ ratio, directly influences the **directionality** of several enzymatic reactions, especially in **glycolysis**, **gluconeogenesis**, and the **citric acid cycle**.

- **High NADH** inhibits **gluconeogenesis** by reducing the availability of **oxaloacetate**, a key intermediate.
- **High NADH** also favors **reductive** reactions over **oxidative** ones.

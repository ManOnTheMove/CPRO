The clinical scenario presents a 25-year-old male with hemoptysis and dark brown urine, consistent with hematuria. Laboratory findings show elevated blood pressure (160/100 mmHg), significant hematuria (12–14 RBCs/HPF), granular and RBC casts in the urine, and renal dysfunction (BUN 28 mg/dL, creatinine 2.5 mg/dL). The key diagnostic feature is the **linear IgG deposition** observed on kidney biopsy immunofluorescence.

---

### Clinical and Pathological Clues

- **Hemoptysis and hematuria** suggest a systemic condition affecting both the lungs and kidneys.
- **Linear IgG deposition** in the glomeruli is **pathognomonic** of **anti-glomerular basement membrane (anti-GBM) antibody disease**, also known as **Goodpasture syndrome** when both pulmonary and renal manifestations are present.
- **RBC casts** in the urine are a hallmark of **glomerulonephritis**, particularly in rapidly progressive glomerulonephritis (RPGN).
- The **absence of proteinuria** in the urinalysis (except for a 24-hour protein of 1 g) and **lack of granular IgG deposition** help rule out immune complex-mediated diseases like lupus nephritis.

---

### Evaluation of Answer Choices

- **A: Anti-neutrophil cytoplasmic antibody (c-ANCA)**  
  - Seen in granulomatosis with polyangiitis (GPA).
  - Typically presents with necrotizing glomerulonephritis and a **negative immunofluorescence** or **granular pattern**, not linear IgG.

- **B: Anti-phospholipid antibody**  
  - Associated with **thrombosis and recurrent pregnancy loss**, not with glomerular basement membrane injury or hemoptysis.

- **C: Anti-neutrophil perinuclear antibody (p-ANCA)**  
  - Seen in microscopic polyangiitis and eosinophilic granulomatosis with polyangiitis (EGPA).
  - Again, these are **vasculitides** with **necrotizing glomerulonephritis**, and **not linear IgG deposition**.

- **D: Anti-glomerular basement membrane antibody (anti-GBM)**  
  - This is the **most consistent** with the clinical and pathological findings.
  - Anti-GBM disease is a **type II hypersensitivity** where autoantibodies directly bind to the **type IV collagen** in the glomerular basement membrane, resulting in **linear IgG staining** on immunofluorescence.
  - The clinical triad of **hemoptysis, hematuria, and renal failure** is classic for **Goodpasture syndrome**.

- **E: Anti-dsDNA antibody**  
  - Seen in **systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE)**.
  - Would show **granular IgG and IgM deposition** on immunofluorescence, not linear.

---

### Conclusion

The presence of **linear IgG immunofluorescence** in the glomeruli, along with pulmonary and renal symptoms, points most strongly to **anti-GBM antibody disease**. This is the **most specific and likely** cause in this patient.

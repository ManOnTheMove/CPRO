### Mechanism of the Side Effect

**Blood transfusions**, particularly **massive or rapid transfusions**, can lead to **citrate toxicity**. Blood products are stored with **citrate**, an anticoagulant that binds **calcium** in the blood. During large-volume transfusions, citrate can outpace the body’s ability to metabolize it, leading to **hypocalcemia**.

Hypocalcemia can manifest as:

- **Tetany** (involuntary muscle contractions, paresthesias)
- Chvostek’s or Trousseau’s signs
- ECG changes (e.g., prolonged QT interval)

Thus, **tetany** is a **direct and clinically relevant side effect** of blood transfusion in the context of sickle cell disease management.

The clinical scenario describes a 56-year-old man with a long-standing history of back issues, stiffness in the cervical spine, kyphosis, and peripheral joint involvement (wrist swelling). These features are classic for **ankylosing spondylitis (AS)**, a type of **spondyloarthropathy** that primarily affects the axial skeleton.

---

### Key Clinical Features of Ankylosing Spondylitis:
- **Chronic back pain and stiffness** for more than 3 months
- **Reduced spinal mobility**, especially in the cervical and lumbar spine
- **Kyphosis** due to fusion of vertebrae
- **Peripheral joint involvement**, such as wrist or knee
- **No pain on active or passive movement** (as the inflammation is more insidious and not acute)
- **Morning stiffness** and fatigue (even with adequate sleep)

These are all consistent with AS, which typically presents in the second or third decade and progresses over time, especially if left untreated.

---

### Treatment Approach in Ankylosing Spondylitis:

The **first-line therapy** for AS is **nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs)**, which are effective in reducing inflammation and improving function in most patients. They are typically used as initial therapy due to their efficacy in controlling symptoms and their relatively low cost and availability.

- **Indomethacin (B)** is a classic NSAID and is a strong candidate for early treatment.
- **Infliximab (A)** is a tumor necrosis factor (TNF) inhibitor used when NSAIDs fail to control symptoms.
- **Methotrexate (D)** and **Sulfasalazine (E)** are more commonly used in **peripheral spondyloarthropathies** (e.g., psoriatic arthritis, reactive arthritis) and **rheumatoid arthritis**, not as first-line for axial disease like AS.
- **Ceftriaxone (C)** is an antibiotic and not relevant for a non-infectious inflammatory condition like AS.

---

### Conclusion:

Given the patient’s clinical presentation and the question focusing on **early treatment**, the most appropriate **first-line therapy** would be a **nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug**.

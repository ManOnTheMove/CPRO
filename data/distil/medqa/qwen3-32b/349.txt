### Clinical Context

An 8-month-old male presents with **painless rectal bleeding** over 5 days and **mild iron deficiency**. No abdominal pain is reported, and the physical and past medical history are unremarkable. These features strongly suggest a **gastrointestinal source of chronic blood loss**, with **Meckel’s diverticulum** being the most likely diagnosis in this age group.

### Understanding Meckel’s Diverticulum

- **Definition**: A congenital outpouching of the ileum, a remnant of the vitelline duct.
- **Prevalence**: Present in **2–4%** of the population, though most are asymptomatic.
- **Symptoms in Infants**:
  - **Painless rectal bleeding** is the most common presentation.
  - Chronic blood loss may lead to **iron deficiency anemia**.
- **Ectopic Tissue**:
  - Often contains **gastric or pancreatic mucosa**, which can lead to ulceration and bleeding due to acid secretion.
- **Diagnosis**:
  - **Meckel’s scan (Tc-99m pertechnetate scan)** is the first-line imaging modality.
  - It detects **ectopic gastric mucosa**, which is a hallmark of Meckel’s diverticulum.

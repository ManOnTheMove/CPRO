### Clinical Interpretation

A TIA is defined as a transient episode of neurological dysfunction caused by focal brain, spinal cord, or retinal ischemia, without acute infarction. The duration of the symptoms in this case (45 minutes) fits within the diagnostic criteria for TIA, and the complete resolution of symptoms is also consistent with this diagnosis.

The absence of residual neurological deficits on physical examination further supports the TIA hypothesis. However, the key point is that **TIA is a medical emergency**, as it often precedes a stroke. The risk of stroke within 48 hours of a TIA is estimated to be as high as 10%, making prompt evaluation and intervention critical.

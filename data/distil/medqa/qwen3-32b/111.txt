### Clinical Context and Understanding

The patient in question is a 70-year-old man diagnosed with **Burkitt lymphoma**, an aggressive B-cell lymphoma known for its rapid proliferation and high sensitivity to chemotherapy. The physician is initiating a **CODOX-M/IVAC chemotherapy regimen**, which is a standard treatment for this type of lymphoma.

A **common and potentially life-threatening complication** of such chemotherapy is **tumor lysis syndrome (TLS)**. TLS occurs when large numbers of tumor cells are lysed rapidly, releasing intracellular contents like potassium, phosphate, uric acid, and other metabolites into the bloodstream. This can lead to **hyperuricemia**, **hyperkalemia**, **hyperphosphatemia**, and **hypocalcemia**, with **acute kidney injury** being a serious consequence.

### Role of Rasburicase

**Rasburicase** is a **recombinant urate oxidase** enzyme that catalyzes the conversion of **uric acid to allantoin**. Allantoin is more water-soluble than uric acid, which allows for **enhanced renal excretion**, thus preventing uric acid nephropathy and other complications of TLS.

This is in contrast to **allopurinol**, a **xanthine oxidase inhibitor**, which prevents the formation of uric acid by blocking the enzyme that converts xanthine to uric acid.

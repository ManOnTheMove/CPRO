### Key Clinical Indicators of SLE:
- **Malar rash** – a classic sign of SLE.
- **Arthralgias** – commonly seen in SLE, especially in the hands, knees, and elbows.
- **Pleuritis** – indicated by the pleural friction rub and left-sided pleural effusion.
- **Renal involvement** – shown by proteinuria on urinalysis.
- **Hematologic abnormalities** – anemia, leukopenia, and thrombocytopenia are common in SLE due to **autoimmune destruction** of blood cells.

### Evaluation of Answer Choices:

- **A: Hematologic malignancy**  
  While malignancies can cause pancytopenia, they typically present with more acute or subacute symptoms, often including lymphadenopathy or splenomegaly. The chronic nature of the symptoms and the presence of a rash and joint involvement make this less likely.

- **B: Immune-mediated destruction** ✅  
  This is the most likely explanation. In SLE, the immune system produces autoantibodies that attack red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets, leading to the observed cytopenias. The presence of a malar rash, joint pain, pleuritis, and proteinuria all support a diagnosis of SLE, where immune-mediated destruction is the primary pathophysiological mechanism.

- **C: Mechanical shearing**  
  This is characteristic of conditions like TTP or HUS, which present with microangiopathic hemolytic anemia, schistocytes on peripheral smear, and thrombocytopenia. These are not consistent with the patient's presentation or lab findings.

- **D: Splenic sequestration**  
  This would lead to isolated thrombocytopenia or anemia in some cases, but not all three cell lines. It does not account for the rash, joint pain, or renal findings.

- **E: Viral infection**  
  While some viral infections can cause transient pancytopenia, the chronic and systemic nature of the symptoms (6-month joint pain, rash, renal involvement) is more consistent with an autoimmune process than a viral etiology.

The most likely explanation for the patient's lab abnormalities is **immune-mediated destruction**, as seen in SLE. The presence of multiple organ system involvement and the characteristic physical findings all support this diagnosis.

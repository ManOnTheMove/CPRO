### Case Summary

A 68-year-old woman presents with symptoms of **fatigue, shortness of breath, and oliguria**. She also reports **morning pedal edema**, a **recent sore throat**, and a **6-month history of back and chest pain** managed with **ibuprofen** and a **laxative**. On physical examination, she is **tachycardic**, **tachypneic**, **hypertensive**, and shows **conjunctival pallor** and **pedal edema**. Laboratory findings reveal **anemia**, **elevated BUN and creatinine (indicating acute kidney injury)**, and **hypercalcemia**. Urinalysis is **negative for protein and blood**.

---

### Key Clinical Clues

1. **Hypercalcemia (serum calcium 12.9 mg/dL)**  
   - Strongly suggests **malignancy**, **hyperparathyroidism**, or **multiple myeloma**.  
   - In the context of renal failure and anemia, **multiple myeloma** becomes a leading suspicion.

2. **Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) with no proteinuria or hematuria**  
   - This is **atypical** for glomerulonephritis (e.g., post-streptococcal, lupus nephritis) or other inflammatory renal diseases.  
   - Suggests **tubular or interstitial pathology**, such as **light chain deposition disease**.

3. **Anemia (Hb 9.8 g/dL)**  
   - Common in **myeloma** due to **bone marrow infiltration** by plasma cells.

4. **History of sore throat followed by symptoms**  
   - Though it could raise suspicion of **post-streptococcal glomerulonephritis**, the **absence of hematuria and proteinuria** makes this **unlikely**.

5. **Family history of SLE**  
   - May be a **red herring**, as the patient's presentation does **not align with lupus nephritis** (e.g., no typical proteinuria, systemic features).

---

### Evaluation of Answer Choices

- **A: Antiglomerular basement membrane antibodies**  
  - Suggests **Goodpasture’s syndrome**, which is characterized by **alveolar hemorrhage** and **rapidly progressive glomerulonephritis** with **hematuria and proteinuria**.  
  - **Not consistent** with the patient's **lack of hematuria or pulmonary symptoms**.

- **B: Renal deposition of AL amyloid**  
  - Can cause **renal dysfunction** and **edema**, but typically presents with **nephrotic syndrome (massive proteinuria)** and **organomegaly**.  
  - **Not supported** by the **absence of significant proteinuria** or other amyloid features.

- **C: Immunoglobulin light chains**  
  - Strongly supports a diagnosis of **multiple myeloma** or **light chain deposition disease** (LCDD), which can cause **renal injury**, **anemia**, and **hypercalcemia**.  
  - The **absence of proteinuria** fits with **tubular injury** from light chain deposition rather than glomerular disease.  
  - **Most consistent** with the clinical and laboratory findings.

- **D: Decreased cardiac ejection fraction**  
  - May explain **edema** and **shortness of breath**, but **does not account** for **AKI**, **hypercalcemia**, or **anemia**.  
  - **Not supported** by the lab data.

- **E: Anti-streptococcal antibodies**  
  - Suggests **post-streptococcal glomerulonephritis**, which typically follows a **group A streptococcal infection** and presents with **hematuria, proteinuria, and hypertension**.  
  - **Not supported** by the **absence of hematuria or proteinuria**.

### Final Diagnosis

The constellation of **acute kidney injury**, **hypercalcemia**, **anemia**, and **absence of proteinuria or hematuria** is most consistent with **multiple myeloma** or **light chain deposition disease**.

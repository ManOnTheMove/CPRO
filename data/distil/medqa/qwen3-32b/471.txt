### Guideline-Based Management

The **Global Initiative for Chronic Obstructive Lung Disease (GOLD)** provides evidence-based recommendations for COPD management based on symptom severity and risk of exacerbations. 

- **GOLD 2 (FEV1 50–80% predicted)** typically involves **mild to moderate symptoms**.
- **Step-up therapy** is indicated when symptoms worsen or when there is an increased use of SABA.
- For **moderate COPD** without a history of frequent exacerbations, the **first-line step-up** is to introduce a **long-acting bronchodilator**.
- The two main classes of long-acting bronchodilators are:
  - **Long-acting beta agonists (LABAs)**
  - **Long-acting muscarinic antagonists (LAMAs)**

According to the GOLD 2023 guidelines, **tiotropium (a LAMA)** is a **first-line add-on therapy** in patients with moderate COPD who are experiencing worsening symptoms and increased use of SABA.

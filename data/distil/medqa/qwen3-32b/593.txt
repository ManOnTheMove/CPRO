### **Mushroom Toxins and Their Mechanisms**

Among the most dangerous mushroom toxins are:
- **Amatoxins** (found in *Amanita phalloides* or "death cap")
- **Gyromitrin** (found in *Gyromitra* species or false morels)
- **Ibotenic acid/muscimol** (found in *Amanita muscaria*)

#### **Amatoxins**
- **Mechanism**: Inhibit **RNA polymerase II**.
- **Effect**: This enzyme is responsible for transcribing mRNA in eukaryotic cells. Its inhibition leads to a halt in protein synthesis, especially in rapidly dividing or highly active cells (like liver and kidney cells).
- **Clinical course**: Initial GI symptoms (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea), followed by a "lull" and then delayed onset of liver and kidney failure.

#### **Gyromitrin**
- **Mechanism**: Converted to methylhydrazine, a potent liver toxin.
- **Effect**: Less directly related to RNA polymerase inhibition.
- **Clinical course**: GI symptoms and potential hemolysis, but not as commonly associated with acute severe dehydration and shock.

#### **Ibotenic acid/muscimol**
- **Mechanism**: Excitotoxic or GABA-mimetic.
- **Effect**: Neurological symptoms (e.g., drowsiness, hallucinations), not primarily GI.

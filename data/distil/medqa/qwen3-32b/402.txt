### Key Components of PCR

1. **Target DNA strand (E)**  
   This is the original DNA template that will be amplified. It is absolutely necessary for PCR to proceed.

2. **Primers (C)**  
   Short DNA sequences that anneal to specific regions on the DNA template. They provide the starting point for DNA synthesis by the polymerase. Without primers, the reaction cannot be targeted and initiated.

3. **Taq polymerase (A)**  
   A heat-stable DNA polymerase enzyme, commonly isolated from *Thermus aquaticus*. It is essential for synthesizing new DNA strands during the extension phase of PCR.

4. **Deoxynucleotide triphosphates (dNTPs) (D)**  
   These are the building blocks used by Taq polymerase to synthesize new DNA strands. Without dNTPs, no DNA replication can occur.

---

### Key Clinical Features Suggesting ITP

- **Chronic, unexplained thrombocytopenia** (6 months duration)
- **Mucocutaneous bleeding** (epistaxis, menorrhagia, purpura)
- **No evidence of systemic illness** (normal vital signs, clear lungs, no fever)
- **Non-palpable purpura** (suggests platelet-related bleeding rather than vasculitis)
- **No schistocytes** expected on blood smear (not microangiopathic hemolytic anemia)

The **son's acute, self-resolving episode** is consistent with **acute ITP in children**, often post-viral. In **adults**, ITP tends to be **chronic**, which fits the timeline and presentation here.

### Laboratory Findings in ITP

- **Bone marrow** (if done): **Normal or increased megakaryocytes**

These findings distinguish ITP from other thrombocytopenic disorders like **TTP** or **DIC**, which involve **microangiopathy** and **abnormal PT/PTT**.

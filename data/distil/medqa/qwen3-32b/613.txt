### Genetic Concept Involved

The key observation here is that the **daughter's disease is more severe and has an earlier age of onset** compared to her father, despite both having the same disease. This is a classic scenario in **trinucleotide repeat disorders**, where the number of **CAG repeats can expand across generations**, particularly in the **germline** (sperm or egg cells).

This phenomenon is known as **anticipation**, and it is characterized by:

- **Earlier age of onset** in successive generations
- **Increased severity** of the disease
- Often linked to **expanding repeat sequences** in genes such as *HTT* in Huntington's disease

The expansion of CAG repeats is **unstable** during DNA replication, especially when the number is already large. This instability leads to **greater expansion in offspring**, particularly when the mutation is inherited from the **father** (in the case of Huntington’s disease).

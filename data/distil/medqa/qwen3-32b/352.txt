### Definitions and Key Relationships

- **Type I Error (α)**: The probability of a **false positive**—incorrectly concluding that a person has the disease when they do not.
- **Type II Error (β)**: The probability of a **false negative**—incorrectly concluding that a person does **not** have the disease when they actually do.
- **Power** = 1 – β: The probability of correctly detecting a true case of the disease.
- **Sensitivity** = 1 – β: The ability of the test to correctly identify those who have the disease.
- **Specificity**: The ability of the test to correctly identify those who do **not** have the disease.

There is an **inverse relationship** between α and β. If you increase the probability of a type I error (α), you generally **decrease** the probability of a type II error (β), and vice versa. This is because making it easier to classify a result as positive (increasing α) reduces the chance of missing true positive cases (reducing β).

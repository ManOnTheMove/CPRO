### Diagnostic Reasoning

**Cushing’s syndrome** is characterized by **excessive cortisol production**, and its etiology can be categorized as follows:

1. **Pituitary-dependent (Cushing’s disease)** – most common cause, usually **responds to high-dose dexamethasone**.
2. **Adrenal tumor (adenoma or carcinoma)** – causes **ectopic cortisol production**, and **inhibits ACTH** (negative feedback), often **does not respond to high-dose dexamethasone**.
3. **Ectopic ACTH syndrome** – **ACTH is secreted from non-pituitary tumors**, most commonly **small cell lung cancer** or other neuroendocrine tumors. These tumors **do not respond to high-dose dexamethasone** and are **not detected on abdominal imaging**.

Given the **lack of suppression with high-dose dexamethasone** and **normal abdominal CT**, the most likely diagnosis is **ectopic ACTH syndrome**.

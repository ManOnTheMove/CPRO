### Mechanism of Action of Methyldopa

Methyldopa is a **centrally acting antihypertensive agent**. It is a **prodrug** that is converted in the brain to **alpha-methyl-norepinephrine**, a compound that mimics the action of norepinephrine.

This metabolite acts as an **agonist at central alpha-2 adrenergic receptors**, specifically in the **brainstem** (medulla oblongata). Activation of these receptors leads to a **reduction in sympathetic outflow** from the central nervous system. This results in:

- **Decreased peripheral vascular resistance**
- **Reduced heart rate**
- **Lowered blood pressure**

This mechanism is **distinct** from direct vasodilation or peripheral receptor blockade.

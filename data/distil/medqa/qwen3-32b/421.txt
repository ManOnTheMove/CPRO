### Mechanism of Action

Varenicline acts as a **selective partial agonist at α4β2 nicotinic acetylcholine receptors**, which are the primary receptors responsible for the reinforcing effects of nicotine. It binds to these receptors more potently than nicotine but produces a weaker stimulation, thereby reducing nicotine cravings and withdrawal symptoms while also diminishing the rewarding effects of smoking.

---

### Side Effects of Varenicline

While varenicline is effective in helping patients quit smoking, it is associated with a number of potential side effects. Among the most notable and clinically significant is its **neuropsychiatric side effect profile**.

The **U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA)** has issued a **black box warning** for varenicline due to reports of **suicidal ideation, depression, and other neuropsychiatric symptoms**, such as hostility, agitation, and insomnia. These effects are relatively rare but are considered **serious and potentially life-threatening**, especially in vulnerable populations.

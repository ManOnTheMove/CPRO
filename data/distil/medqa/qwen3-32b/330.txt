To determine the correct answer to this question, we begin by analyzing the clinical presentation of the patient. The individual is experiencing **painless, profuse, watery diarrhea** shortly after arriving in Morocco. The stool is described as **white and cloudy**, and the physical exam reveals **signs of significant dehydration** (e.g., dry mouth, decreased skin turgor, hypotension, tachycardia). These findings are **highly suggestive of cholera**, caused by *Vibrio cholerae*, and the characteristic "rice-water" stool further supports this diagnosis.

### Mechanism of Cholera Toxin

The **cholera toxin** is a key virulence factor in this disease. It acts by **ADP-ribosylating** the **Gsα subunit** of G-protein-coupled receptors. This modification **inactivates the GTPase activity**, causing the G protein to remain in a constantly activated state. The result is **persistent activation of adenylyl cyclase**, leading to a **massive increase in intracellular cAMP**. Elevated cAMP levels cause **excessive secretion of chloride and water into the intestinal lumen**, resulting in the **profuse, isotonic diarrhea** typical of cholera.

---

### Comparing with Other Toxins

Let’s now evaluate the mechanisms of the listed toxins to identify which one is **most similar** to the **cholera toxin**:

- **A. Diphtheria toxin**: ADP-ribosylates **elongation factor 2 (EF-2)** in eukaryotic cells, inhibiting protein synthesis. While it uses ADP-ribosylation, the **target is different** (EF-2 vs. G protein), and the **physiological effects are distinct** (cell death vs. ion channel activation).

- **B. Exotoxin A (from *Pseudomonas aeruginosa*)**: This toxin **also ADP-ribosylates the Gsα subunit**, similar to cholera toxin. It **inactivates the GTPase activity**, leading to **increased cAMP levels** and **cellular dysfunction**, particularly in eukaryotic cells. This **mechanism is nearly identical** to that of cholera toxin.

- **C. Botulinum toxin**: Acts by **cleaving SNARE proteins**, which are essential for neurotransmitter release. This causes **flaccid paralysis**, and the mechanism is **entirely different**.

- **D. Edema toxin (from *Bordetella pertussis*)**: While it causes **edema and increased cAMP**, it **activates adenylate cyclase directly**, not by ADP-ribosylation of G proteins. Therefore, the **mechanism is not similar** to cholera toxin.

- **E. Shiga-like toxin**: This toxin **inhibits protein synthesis** by **cleaving the 28S rRNA** in the 60S ribosomal subunit. It is **not related** to the G-protein signaling pathway.

---

### Conclusion

The **cholera toxin** and **Exotoxin A** both **ADP-ribosylate the Gsα subunit**, leading to **persistent activation of adenylyl cyclase and increased cAMP levels**. This makes **Exotoxin A** the toxin with a **mechanism of action most similar** to cholera toxin.

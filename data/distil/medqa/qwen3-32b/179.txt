### Case Summary

A 4-year-old boy is being treated for suspected **Kawasaki disease** with **intravenous immunoglobulin (IVIG)** and **aspirin**. Shortly afterward, he develops **anaphylactic symptoms** including **facial flushing**, **stridor**, **hypotension**, **tachycardia**, and a **pruritic rash**. These symptoms suggest a **severe allergic reaction**, likely triggered by the IVIG. The question is asking which **underlying immunodeficiency** could predispose a patient to such a reaction.

---

### Key Clinical Insight

**IVIG** contains a mixture of **immunoglobulins**, including **IgA**, **IgG**, and **IgM**. In patients with **IgA deficiency**, the body may produce **anti-IgA antibodies** in response to the IgA in IVIG. When such patients receive IVIG, these **anti-IgA antibodies** can bind to the **IgA in the IVIG**, leading to **complement activation**, **mast cell degranulation**, and potentially **anaphylaxis**.

This is a **well-documented clinical scenario**, and **IgA deficiency** is the **most common primary immunodeficiency** associated with **anaphylactoid reactions to IVIG**.

---

### Evaluation of Other Options

- **A. Bruton's agammaglobulinemia**  
  This is a severe **X-linked B-cell deficiency**, resulting in **very low levels of all immunoglobulins**, including IgA. However, the absence of IgA does not usually lead to **anaphylaxis** because **anti-IgA antibodies** are not typically present in this condition.

- **C. Hyper-IgM syndrome**  
  Characterized by **normal or elevated IgM** and **low IgG and IgA**. However, again, **anaphylaxis due to IgA is not a feature** of this syndrome.

- **D. Common variable immunodeficiency (CVID)**  
  Patients may have **low IgA**, but **not necessarily IgA deficiency** in the sense of **total absence** or presence of **anti-IgA antibodies**. Also, **anaphylactic reactions to IVIG** are **rare** in CVID.

- **E. Hyper-IgD syndrome**  
  This is a **periodic fever syndrome** associated with **mutations in mevalonate kinase**. It is **not related** to **IVIG anaphylaxis** or **IgA deficiency**.

---

### Final Answer

The most likely underlying condition that would cause a **severe allergic reaction to IVIG** is **IgA deficiency**, due to the presence of **anti-IgA antibodies** that trigger an **anaphylactoid reaction** upon exposure to IgA in the IVIG product.

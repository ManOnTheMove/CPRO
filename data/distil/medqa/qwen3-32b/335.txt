### Interpretation of Hemoglobin Electrophoresis Results

The hemoglobin electrophoresis results for this 28-year-old male are as follows:

- **HbA (Adult Hemoglobin)**: 93%  
- **HbF (Fetal Hemoglobin)**: 2%  
- **HbA2 (Hemoglobin A2)**: 5%  

---

### Normal Hemoglobin Levels in Adults

To interpret these values, it's important to understand the typical reference ranges for adult hemoglobin:

- **HbA**: 95–98%
- **HbA2**: 2–3%
- **HbF**: <1%

In this case, HbA is slightly reduced, HbA2 is elevated, and HbF is mildly increased. These findings are characteristic of a **beta-globin chain deficiency**, as seen in **beta-thalassemia**.

---

### Differential Diagnosis

#### **A: Sickle Cell Trait**
- Characterized by **HbS** (sickle hemoglobin) at ~40–50% and normal HbA levels.
- **No HbS** is mentioned here, so this is **not consistent** with the findings.

#### **B: Alpha Thalassemia Major with Hb Bart's**
- This is a **severe, often lethal** condition in the neonatal period due to **Hb Bart's (γ⁴)**.
- The patient is 28 years old and has **HbF** (α²γ²), **not Hb Bart's**.
- This **rules out** alpha thalassemia major.

#### **C: Beta-Thalassemia Minor (Beta-Thalassemia Trait)**
- In heterozygous beta-thalassemia:
  - **HbA is reduced** (often 70–95%)
  - **HbA2 is elevated** (>3.5%)
  - **HbF is mildly increased** (1–5%)
- The results here (93% HbA, 5% HbA2, 2% HbF) **fit this pattern**.
- This is the **most likely diagnosis**.

#### **D: Beta-Thalassemia Major**
- Characterized by:
  - **Severe microcytic anemia**
  - **Very high HbF levels** (often >30–90%)
  - **Almost no HbA**
- The patient has **only 2% HbF**, which is **inconsistent** with beta-thalassemia major.

#### **E: Diabetes Mellitus**
- This is unrelated to hemoglobin electrophoresis.
- **HbA1c** is used for monitoring diabetes, but **HbA** and **HbF** levels are not diagnostic of diabetes.
- Therefore, this is **not relevant** in this context.

The combination of **reduced HbA**, **elevated HbA2**, and **mildly increased HbF** is **most consistent with beta-thalassemia minor**, a heterozygous form of beta-thalassemia that typically presents with mild anemia and no significant symptoms.

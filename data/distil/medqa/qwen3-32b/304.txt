**Key Findings and Their Implications:**

- **Hematuria and proteinuria**: These are classic signs of glomerulonephritis. The presence of blood and protein in the urine together is more specific for a glomerular origin.
- **Edema and weight gain**: Suggest hypoalbuminemia due to heavy protein loss in urine, a hallmark of nephrotic syndrome.
- **Unexplained bruising**: May indicate a coagulopathy, possibly secondary to hypoalbuminemia or an underlying systemic illness.
- **Mild hypertension (135/85 mmHg)**: While not severe, it is elevated from his baseline and may reflect early renal dysfunction or a response to volume overload.

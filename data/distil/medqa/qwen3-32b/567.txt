To determine the mechanism by which **Enterotoxigenic *E. coli* (ETEC)** causes diarrhea, it's essential to understand the pathophysiology of this particular type of *E. coli* and the toxins it produces.

---

### Pathogenesis of ETEC

ETEC is a leading cause of **traveler's diarrhea**, especially in regions with poor sanitation. It does not invade the intestinal mucosa but instead **adheres to the intestinal epithelium** and **produces two main enterotoxins**:

1. **Heat-Labile Toxin (LT)**  
2. **Heat-Stable Toxin (ST)**

---

### Mechanism of Diarrhea

- **LT (Heat-Labile Toxin)** mimics the mechanism of **cholera toxin**. It acts by **stimulating adenylate cyclase**, leading to a **significant increase in intracellular cAMP levels** in intestinal epithelial cells. This causes **excessive secretion of water and electrolytes** into the intestinal lumen, resulting in **watery diarrhea**.

- **ST (Heat-Stable Toxin)** works by **activating guanylate cyclase**, which increases intracellular **cGMP**, also leading to fluid secretion. However, **option E** in the question refers specifically to **adenylate cyclase activation**, which is the mechanism associated with **LT**, and this is the more commonly emphasized mechanism in ETEC-related diarrhea in basic medical education.

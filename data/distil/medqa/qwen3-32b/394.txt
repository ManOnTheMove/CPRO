### Diagnostic Approach

In the acute setting of a suspected hernia, especially in adults, **ultrasonography** is the **preferred initial diagnostic test**. Here’s why:

- **Non-invasive and readily available** in most emergency departments.
- **High sensitivity and specificity** for identifying hernia contents (e.g., bowel loops, omentum) and assessing for **strangulation** (e.g., absence of testicular blood flow).
- Can help **differentiate between a reducible and incarcerated hernia**, and may guide surgical planning.
- Avoids the radiation exposure of CT and is more cost-effective than laparoscopy in the diagnostic phase.

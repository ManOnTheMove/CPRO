### Understanding Oxygen Delivery

Oxygen delivery (DO₂) to tissues is calculated as:

$$
DO_2 = CO \times CaO_2
$$

Where:
- **CO** is cardiac output (blood flow),
- **CaO₂** is the arterial oxygen content.

In this question, we are not being asked to reduce blood flow (CO), but rather to reduce oxygen **availability at the tissue level**, i.e., the amount of oxygen **delivered and released** from hemoglobin to the tissues.

This involves two main mechanisms:
1. **Reducing the oxygen content** in the blood (e.g., by reducing hemoglobin or oxygen saturation).
2. **Reducing oxygen unloading** from hemoglobin to tissues (i.e., shifting the oxygen-hemoglobin dissociation curve to the *left*).

### Key Consideration: Prophylaxis for Opportunistic Infections

When patients are treated with **high-dose corticosteroids** and **immunosuppressive agents** like **cyclophosphamide**, they become **immunocompromised**. One of the most significant complications in this setting is the risk of **Pneumocystis jirovecii pneumonia (PCP)**, a potentially life-threatening opportunistic infection.

#### **Trimethoprim/Sulfamethoxazole (TMP/SMX)** is the **first-line prophylactic agent** for PCP in patients receiving immunosuppressive therapy. It is also effective against **Pseudomonas aeruginosa**, a common pathogen in GPA-related sinusitis and lung disease. However, in this context, the more critical indication is **PCP prophylaxis**.

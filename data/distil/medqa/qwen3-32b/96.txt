### Pathophysiology in Sickle Cell Disease

In patients with **sickle cell disease**, the **bone marrow is often hyperactive** due to **chronic hemolysis**, and **reticulocyte counts are typically elevated**. However, in the setting of an **aplastic crisis**, the bone marrow's ability to produce RBCs is **abruptly suppressed**.

The **most common cause of aplastic crisis in sickle cell disease is infection with Parvovirus B19**. This virus specifically **targets erythroid progenitor cells** in the bone marrow, leading to **a temporary cessation of RBC production**.

---

### Understanding the Pathophysiology

Overflow incontinence occurs when the bladder cannot empty adequately due to obstruction or poor detrusor function, leading to continuous or intermittent leakage of urine from an overfilled bladder. In this case, the distended bladder and history of BPH are key indicators of **obstructive urinary retention**.

---

  While **transurethral resection of the prostate (TURP)** is a definitive treatment for BPH, it is **not the first-line initial management**, especially when the patient is stable and can be managed medically or with catheterization first.

  This is an **alpha-1 adrenergic blocker** that relaxes the smooth muscle in the prostate and bladder neck. - **E: Pelvic floor muscle training**

This case presents a 4-year-old boy with a history of multiple falls, delayed walking (until 18 months), marked calf enlargement, and the use of a characteristic "Gowers' maneuver" to stand from a seated position. These findings are classic for **Duchenne muscular dystrophy (DMD)**.

---

### Clinical Features and Their Significance

- **Delayed walking (18 months)**: DMD often presents with delayed motor milestones due to progressive muscle weakness.
- **Multiple falls**: Muscle weakness, especially in the proximal muscles of the lower limbs, leads to instability and frequent falls.
- **Enlarged calves (pseudohypertrophy)**: In DMD, the calves appear enlarged due to the replacement of muscle with fatty and fibrous tissue, not true hypertrophy.
- **Gowers' sign**: The child uses his hands to "walk" up his legs to stand, indicating proximal muscle weakness in the lower limbs.

These are hallmark features of **DMD**, a severe, progressive, X-linked recessive disorder.

---

### Pathophysiology and Genetic Basis

- **Duchenne muscular dystrophy** is caused by a **mutation or absence of the dystrophin protein**, which is essential for muscle fiber integrity.
- Without dystrophin, muscle fibers are more susceptible to injury during contraction, leading to progressive muscle degeneration and weakness.

- **E. SMN1 gene defect**: Causes **spinal muscular atrophy (SMA)**, a motor neuron disease that does not present with calf pseudohypertrophy or Gowers' sign.

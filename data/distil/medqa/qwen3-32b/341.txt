### Clinical Context and Differential Diagnosis

- **Sickle cell disease** predisposes patients to **avascular necrosis (AVN)** due to occlusion of small vessels by sickled red blood cells, especially in the femoral head.
- **Hip pain with limping and limited range of motion** in a child with sickle cell is **highly suggestive of AVN**.

### Diagnostic Approach

- **Radiograph (X-ray)** is often the first-line imaging modality for evaluating musculoskeletal pain. However, in **early AVN**, **X-rays may appear normal** for several months after the onset of vascular compromise.

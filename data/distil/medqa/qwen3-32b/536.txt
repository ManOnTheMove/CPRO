The Darrow-Yannet diagram is a widely used graphical representation in clinical physiology, particularly for analyzing disturbances in body fluid homeostasis. It provides a visual depiction of how changes in extracellular fluid (ECF) volume and osmolality affect the body's compensatory mechanisms.

---

### Understanding the Axes of the Darrow-Yannet Diagram

- **X-axis**: Represents **ECF volume**. It reflects the overall status of the extracellular compartment, whether it is in volume excess, volume depletion, or normal.
- **Y-axis**: Represents **ECF osmolality**, indicating the concentration of solutes in the extracellular fluid.

This diagram is especially useful in diagnosing and understanding disorders such as hyponatremia, hypernatremia, and conditions involving fluid retention or loss (e.g., heart failure, SIADH, dehydration).

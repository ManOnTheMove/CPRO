### Understanding the Pharmacological Mechanism

When an **α1 adrenergic agonist** is instilled in the eye, it primarily affects the **pupillary dilator muscle**. This muscle is innervated by the **sympathetic nervous system**, and it contracts in response to **α1-adrenergic stimulation**, leading to **mydriasis** (pupil dilation). This is a classic and expected effect of such agents in ophthalmic use.

In contrast, the **pupillary sphincter muscle**, which is innervated by the **parasympathetic nervous system**, is not affected by α1 agonists. It is more responsive to **cholinergic stimulation** or **anticholinergic blockade**.

The **ciliary muscle**, which is also innervated by the **parasympathetic nervous system**, is responsible for accommodation. It is **not significantly affected** by α1 adrenergic agonists. These agents do not typically induce **cycloplegia** (loss of accommodation) or cause contraction of the ciliary muscle.

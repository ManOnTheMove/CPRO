---

### Ethical Considerations

The principle of **autonomy** is central to modern medical ethics. It asserts that **competent patients have the right to be informed about their medical condition and to make decisions regarding their care**. Even in cultures where family members traditionally assume a role in decision-making, the patient still retains the right to understand and participate in decisions affecting their health.

Using **family members as interpreters** (Option A) is often discouraged in clinical settings due to potential biases, lack of medical terminology knowledge, and the emotional context that may alter the information being conveyed. This can lead to incomplete or inaccurate communication.

---

### Language and Communication

Since the patient does not speak much English, **Option D (speaking directly in English)** is not viable. Effective communication is essential for informed consent and shared decision-making. **Option E (transferring care to a Spanish-speaking colleague)** may help with language, but it does not resolve the core issue of ensuring the patient fully understands their condition and the proposed interventions.

---

### Best Practice in Medical Communication

The **use of a professional interpreter** (Option B) is the **gold standard** in such situations. A trained interpreter ensures that:

- Medical information is **accurately translated** without interpretation or omission.
- The patient's **questions and concerns are fully addressed**.
- The patient is empowered to **make autonomous decisions**.
- Cultural differences are **acknowledged and respected**, but not allowed to override the patient's right to self-determination.

This approach also allows the physician to explain the situation to the family in a culturally sensitive way, emphasizing the importance of the patient's right to be informed.

---

### Why Other Options Fall Short

- **Option C** (not discussing the plan with the patient) violates the principle of autonomy and is ethically inappropriate.
- **Option A** risks miscommunication and lacks the safeguards provided by a professional interpreter.
- **Option D** is ineffective due to the language barrier.
- **Option E** is impractical and does not guarantee that the patient will be fully informed or involved in the decision-making process.

---

### Conclusion

The physician should prioritize the patient's right to be informed and involved in their care by using a **professional Spanish-speaking interpreter**.

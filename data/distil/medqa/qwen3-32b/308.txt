### Clinical Scenario
A 14-year-old male with **polyuria** and **unintentional weight loss** despite **increased caloric intake** presents for a well-child checkup. These symptoms are **classic signs of diabetes mellitus**, particularly **Type 1 diabetes mellitus (T1DM)**.

### Understanding the Pathophysiology
- **Type 1 diabetes** is an **autoimmune disease** in which the immune system mistakenly attacks and destroys the **insulin-producing beta-cells** of the pancreas.
- The destruction of these cells leads to **absolute insulin deficiency**, resulting in:
  - **Hyperglycemia** (elevated blood sugar),
  - **Polyuria** (frequent urination due to osmotic diuresis),
  - **Polyphagia** (increased hunger),
  - **Weight loss** (due to lack of glucose utilization and breakdown of fat and muscle for energy).

  ✅ **Correct**. The hallmark of T1DM is **autoimmune destruction** of pancreatic beta-cells by **self-reactive T-cells**, leading to insulin deficiency. This is directly consistent with the clinical scenario.

### Case Summary

A 35-year-old sexually active woman presents with **recurrent oral ulcers** (third episode in 8 months), **dyspareunia**, and **uveitis**. These symptoms suggest an underlying **systemic autoimmune or inflammatory condition** with mucocutaneous and ocular manifestations.

---

### Clinical Considerations

The key features in this patient's presentation are:

- **Recurrent oral ulcers**
- **Dyspareunia** (which may suggest genital ulcers)
- **Uveitis**

These findings are **highly suggestive of Behçet's disease**, a multisystem inflammatory disorder characterized by:

- **Recurrent oral and genital ulcers**
- **Uveitis**
- **Skin lesions**
- **Arthralgias**, **vasculitis**, and **central nervous system involvement** in some cases

Behçet's disease is **not organ-specific** and typically does **not involve antinuclear antibodies (ANA)** or **anti-dsDNA**, which are more commonly associated with **systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE)**.

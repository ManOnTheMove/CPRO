### Differentiating Types of Shock

Given the clinical context, we must consider:

- **Hypovolemic shock**: Unlikely, as the patient has **distended jugular veins**, which suggests **not volume depletion**.

In **cardiogenic shock**, the left ventricle fails to eject blood effectively, leading to **pulmonary congestion and backup of venous return**, which is reflected as **elevated central venous pressure (CVP)**.

Her history of **breast cancer**, treated with **radiation** and **aggressive chemotherapy**, raises the possibility of **malignant pericardial effusion**, a known complication of advanced or recurrent cancer.

### Physical and Diagnostic Findings

- **Pulsus paradoxus** is a hallmark of cardiac tamponade. It reflects the **respiratory variation in venous return**, where **inspiration** increases right-sided filling and decreases left-sided filling due to **pressure transmission across the non-compliant pericardium**.
- **Elevated JVP** and **distant heart sounds** further support this diagnosis.
- **Echocardiography** is the gold standard for confirming cardiac tamponade and evaluating ventricular filling dynamics.

---

### Echocardiographic Features of Cardiac Tamponade

In cardiac tamponade, the key echocardiographic findings include:

- **Pericardial effusion** (which may be loculated).
- **Right atrial or right ventricular diastolic collapse** during inspiration.
- **Respiratory variation in ventricular filling**:
  - **Increased right ventricular filling** with **decreased left ventricular filling** during inspiration.
  - This leads to a **respiratory variation in mitral and tricuspid inflow velocities**.
- **Doppler flow patterns** show **exaggerated respiratory variation** in **inferior vena cava (IVC) flow** and **atrioventricular valve inflow**.

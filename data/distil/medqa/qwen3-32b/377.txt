### Interpretation of Clinical Features

#### 1. **Facial Dysmorphology**
The **broad nasal bridge, short philtrum, and micrognathia** are **classic facial features** of **22q11.2 deletion syndrome (DiGeorge syndrome)**. These features are **not typically seen in other chromosomal abnormalities** such as **Down syndrome (trisomy 21)** or **Cri-du-chat syndrome (5p deletion)**.

#### 2. **Cleft Palate**
Cleft palate is a **well-documented feature** of **22q11.2 deletion syndrome**, especially when isolated or in combination with **micrognathia**, indicating **velopharyngeal insufficiency**.

#### 3. **Muscle Spasms**
The **intermittent muscle spasms**, particularly in the **hands and feet**, suggest **hypocalcemia-induced tetany**. This is a **hallmark of DiGeorge syndrome**, due to **hypoplastic or aplastic parathyroid glands**, which results in **low calcium levels** in the neonatal period.

#### 4. **Cardiac Murmur**
A **harsh systolic murmur over the lower left sternal border** is consistent with **ventricular septal defect (VSD)** or **other conotruncal heart defects**, which are **common in 22q11.2 deletion syndrome**. These include **tetralogy of Fallot, truncus arteriosus, and interrupted aortic arch**.

### Hemoglobin and Oxygen Affinity

Hemoglobin exists in two primary conformational states:

- **Relaxed (R) state**: High oxygen affinity — hemoglobin holds oxygen tightly.
- **Taut (T) state**: Low oxygen affinity — hemoglobin releases oxygen more readily.

In the lungs, where oxygen levels are high, hemoglobin adopts the R state to bind oxygen efficiently. In peripheral tissues, especially active muscles, oxygen levels are low, and conditions such as **low pH (acidosis)**, **high CO₂**, and **high 2,3-BPG** promote the T state, facilitating oxygen unloading.

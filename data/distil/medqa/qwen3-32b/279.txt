### Pathophysiology of Hepatic Encephalopathy

Hepatic encephalopathy occurs in the setting of **liver dysfunction**, particularly **cirrhosis**, where the liver is unable to effectively **detoxify ammonia** from the gut. Ammonia, a neurotoxin, crosses the blood-brain barrier and disrupts normal brain function, leading to a spectrum of neuropsychiatric symptoms ranging from subtle confusion to coma.

The **treatment goal** is to reduce **ammonia levels**, which is typically achieved using **lactulose**.

---

### Mechanism of Lactulose

- **Lactulose** is a **non-absorbable disaccharide**.
- It is fermented in the colon by gut bacteria into **organic acids**, which **acidify the colonic environment**.
- In an acidic milieu, **ammonia (NH₃)** is converted to **ammonium (NH₄⁺)**, a **non-diffusible ion** that is **trapped in the colon** and excreted in the stool.
- This **reduces systemic ammonia levels**, thereby improving hepatic encephalopathy.

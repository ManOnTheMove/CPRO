Waardenburg’s syndrome is a group of autosomal dominant genetic disorders that primarily affect pigmentation and hearing. It is most commonly associated with abnormalities in melanocyte migration during embryonic development, leading to a wide range of clinical features.

---

### Key Features of Waardenburg’s Syndrome:

1. **Heterochromia (A)**  
   This is a hallmark of the syndrome and may present as heterochromia iridum (differently colored eyes) or sectoral heterochromia (part of one eye is a different color).

2. **Hair Hypopigmentation (B)**  
   A common feature is a white forelock (patch of white hair at the front of the scalp), and sometimes other areas of hypopigmented hair.

3. **Hearing Loss**  
   Hearing loss in Waardenburg syndrome is **sensorineural**, not conductive. It results from developmental abnormalities of the inner ear. Therefore, **conductive hearing loss (C)** is **not** a feature of Waardenburg’s syndrome.

4. **Lateral Displacement of Inner Canthi (D)**  
   This is referred to as **dystopia canthorum** and is a classic facial feature in some subtypes, especially Type 1.

5. **Broad Nasal Root (E)**  
   This is also a common facial feature in individuals with Waardenburg syndrome, particularly in Type 1 and Type 3.

---

### Conclusion:

Among the listed features, **conductive hearing loss (C)** is not associated with Waardenburg’s syndrome. The hearing loss seen in this condition is sensorineural in nature.

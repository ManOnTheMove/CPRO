### Overview of 5-Fluorouracil (5-FU) and Its Toxicities

5-FU is a pyrimidine analog that acts as an antimetabolite, interfering with DNA synthesis and RNA function. It is commonly used in the treatment of colorectal, breast, and other gastrointestinal cancers. Among its key toxicities is **myelosuppression**, which affects all three major bone marrow cell lines:

- **Anemia** (low red blood cells)
- **Thrombocytopenia** (low platelets)
- **Neutropenia** (low neutrophils)

Neutropenia is particularly clinically significant because it increases the risk of infection, a potentially life-threatening complication during chemotherapy.

---

### Evaluation of Monitoring Options

- **A: Creatinine**  
  Creatinine is a marker of kidney function. While some chemotherapy drugs (e.g., cisplatin) can cause nephrotoxicity, 5-FU is not primarily associated with renal toxicity. Therefore, creatinine monitoring is not a routine requirement for 5-FU therapy.

- **B: Fecal leukocytes**  
  Fecal leukocytes are used to detect inflammatory bowel disease or bacterial infection (e.g., *Clostridioides difficile*). While 5-FU can cause diarrhea, it is not typically associated with infectious colitis, and this test is not part of routine monitoring for 5-FU.

- **C: Monofilament testing**  
  Monofilament testing is used to assess peripheral neuropathy, commonly seen with drugs like **oxaliplatin**, not 5-FU. Thus, this is not a relevant monitoring parameter in this context.

  This is the correct answer. Neutrophil count (often part of a complete blood count with differential) is **a critical parameter to monitor** during 5-FU therapy due to the risk of **neutropenia**. Regular monitoring allows for early detection and management of this potentially serious side effect, including dose adjustments or holding therapy if necessary.

- **E: Peak flow**  
  Peak flow is used to assess respiratory function in asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). 5-FU is not associated with pulmonary toxicity, making this an irrelevant parameter in this scenario.

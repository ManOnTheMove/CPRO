### Understanding the Pathophysiology

In **type 2 diabetes**, chronic hyperglycemia leads to a cascade of renal changes, including:

1. **Early glomerular hyperfiltration** – This is an initial compensatory response to high glucose levels, characterized by increased glomerular pressure and flow. This hyperfiltration is a key early event in the development of diabetic nephropathy.

2. **Progression to glomerular damage** – Over time, the increased glomerular pressure causes structural damage, such as **basement membrane thickening**, **mesangial expansion**, and **nodular glomerulosclerosis (Kimmelstiel-Wilson lesions)**. These structural changes increase glomerular permeability, leading to **proteinuria**.

3. **Glucosuria** – In this case, the presence of **glucose in the urine** is a result of **hyperglycemia exceeding the renal threshold for glucose reabsorption**, which is expected in poorly controlled diabetes.

---

1. **Pulmonary Function Findings**:
   - A **decreased FVC** with a **normal or increased FEV1/FVC ratio** is characteristic of **restrictive lung disease**.
   - This is in contrast to **obstructive lung diseases** (e.g., COPD, asthma), where FEV1/FVC is reduced.

2. **Clinical and Occupational History**:
   - The patient worked in various industries, increasing the likelihood of **asbestos exposure**.
   - Asbestos is a known **pneumoconiotic agent** that leads to **asbestosis**, a type of **interstitial lung disease**.

3. **Pathology Findings**:
   - **Macrophages with dark, round particles** in the **upper lobes** is a classic finding in **asbestosis**.
   - Asbestos fibers are long and thin, but when phagocytized by macrophages, they may appear as **dark, granular inclusions** on histology.

4. **Disease Pattern**:
   - Asbestosis is typically **upper lobe predominant** and causes **interstitial fibrosis**, leading to **restrictive lung disease**.
   - The patient's symptoms are consistent with **progressive dyspnea on exertion**, a hallmark of **asbestosis**.

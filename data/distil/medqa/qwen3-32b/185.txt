### **Differential Diagnosis**
Given the presence of a Y chromosome and normal testes and testosterone levels, the most likely explanation for the **ambiguous genitalia** is a **disorder of androgen action or conversion**, not a defect in testosterone production.

Two main possibilities:
1. **Androgen Insensitivity Syndrome (AIS)**: A defect in the androgen receptor, leading to decreased response to androgens. In this case, testosterone would still be normal (as the testes are producing it), but the tissues would not respond appropriately.
2. **5α-Reductase Deficiency**: A defect in the enzyme responsible for converting testosterone to **dihydrotestosterone (DHT)**, a more potent androgen essential for external genital development.

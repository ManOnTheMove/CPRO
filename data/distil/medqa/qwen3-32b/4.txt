### Immediate Priorities in DKA Management

In managing DKA, a systematic approach is required, with the following **prioritized steps**:

1. **Fluid Resuscitation (Hypoperfusion)**
   - DKA leads to **significant dehydration** (up to 6–10 L of fluid loss).
   - **Hypotension and tachycardia** suggest **hypovolemic shock**.
   - Initial management is to restore intravascular volume with **isotonic saline (0.9% NaCl)** at **1–2 L/hour** for the first hour.
   - Correcting **hypoperfusion** is **critical** to prevent **multi-organ failure** and to allow the body to respond effectively to insulin and other treatments.

2. **Insulin Therapy (Hyperglycemia)**
   - Insulin is used to **reduce glucose** and **inhibit ketogenesis**.
   - However, **insulin cannot be effective** in a patient with **poor perfusion**, as cellular uptake is impaired.

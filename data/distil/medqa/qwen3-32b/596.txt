### Clinical Overview

This case presents a 15-year-old girl with a **3-day history of lower abdominal pain**, **recurring episodes over 9 months**, and **primary amenorrhea** (no menarche). On physical examination, she is **Tanner stage 4** in both pubic hair and breast development, indicating that she is **close to or at the expected age for menarche**. The **pelvic exam reveals bulging, bluish tissue 1 cm inside the vaginal introitus**, and there is **suprapubic tenderness**. 

---

### Key Clinical Clues

- **Primary amenorrhea** at 15 years with **normal secondary sexual characteristics** (Tanner 4) suggests the reproductive tract is otherwise functional, at least in terms of hormone production.
- **Cyclic abdominal pain** suggests a **periodic process**, such as **menstruation**, which is being **obstructed**.
- **Bulging, bluish tissue at the vaginal introitus** is **highly suggestive of a hematocolpos** — a collection of menstrual blood behind a physical obstruction.
- **Suprapubic tenderness** may result from **pressure buildup** due to this obstruction.

---

### Differential Diagnosis

1. **Imperforate Hymen**  
   - This is the most likely diagnosis.
   - An **imperforate hymen** is a **congenital anomaly** where the hymen is intact and covers the entire vaginal opening.
   - Menstrual blood cannot exit, leading to **cyclic accumulation** (hematocolpos), causing **cyclic lower abdominal pain**.
   - The **bluish bulge** is due to **blood pooling** behind the hymen.
   - This condition is **asymptomatic until menarche**, when **cyclic pain** and **abdominal distension** occur.
   - It is **easily diagnosed on pelvic exam** and **treated with hymenotomy**.

2. **Labial Adhesions**  
   - More common in **prepubertal girls**, not typically associated with **cyclic pain** or **bulging hymenal tissue**.
   - Usually presents with **fusion of the labia minora**, not obstruction of menstrual flow.

3. **Androgen Insensitivity Syndrome (AIS)**  
   - Patients are **46,XY**, with **female external genitalia** due to androgen resistance.
   - **No uterus or upper vagina**, **absent breast development** (unless estrogen is present), and **inguinal hernias** with testes may be present.
   - **No bluish bulge**, and **menstruation would not occur** due to absence of uterus.

4. **Müllerian Agenesis (Rokitansky-Küster-Hauser syndrome)**  
   - Absence of the **uterus and upper 2/3 of the vagina**, with **normal ovaries and secondary sexual characteristics**.
   - **No hymen or vaginal introitus**, so **no bluish bulge** would be present.
   - Menstruation does not occur due to **lack of a uterus**.

5. **Endometriosis**  
   - Causes **cyclic pelvic pain**, but typically after **menarche**.
   - **No bluish bulge**, and **not associated with hematocolpos**.
   - Less likely in a premenarchal or early postmenarchal girl.

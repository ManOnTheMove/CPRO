### Evaluation of the Options:

- **A: Subcutaneous terbutaline**  
  Terbutaline is a short-acting beta-agonist (SABA), typically used for **acute bronchospasm relief**. It is **not** a controller medication and is contraindicated for this use case. **Eliminated**.

- **B: Inhaled ipratropium bromide**  
  Ipratropium is an anticholinergic bronchodilator. While it may be used in combination with beta-agonists during **acute exacerbations**, it is **not a long-term controller medication**. It can provide some **short-term bronchodilation**, so it's not suitable for **prophylaxis only**. **Eliminated**.

- **C: Oral theophylline**  
  Theophylline is a **methylxanthine** that can be used as a **maintenance medication** in asthma. It has a **bronchodilator effect** and can be used in **moderate asthma** when ICS are not tolerated. However, it has a **narrow therapeutic index**, requires **frequent monitoring**, and **can provide some bronchodilation in acute settings**, making it **less ideal** as a prophylactic-only agent. **Less preferred**.

- **D: Inhaled cromolyn sodium**  
  Cromolyn is a **mast cell stabilizer**. It **prevents the release of inflammatory mediators** from mast cells and is used as a **prophylactic agent**, particularly in **mild persistent asthma** and **exercise-induced bronchoconstriction**. - **E: Oral roflumilast**  
  Roflumilast is a **phosphodiesterase-4 (PDE4) inhibitor**, approved for the **treatment of COPD**, not asthma, and especially **not in children**. It is **not indicated** in this context. **Eliminated**.

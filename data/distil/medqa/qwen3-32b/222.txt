### Differential Diagnosis

The most likely cause of iron deficiency in an older adult is **chronic blood loss**, particularly from the **gastrointestinal tract**. Other causes, such as **malabsorption** or **dietary deficiency**, are less likely in the absence of relevant symptoms or history.

---

In patients with **unexplained iron deficiency anemia**, especially in **older adults**, it is crucial to **investigate for a source of chronic blood loss**. The primary concern in this age group is **malignancy**, particularly **colorectal cancer**, which is a common and potentially life-threatening cause of occult GI bleeding.

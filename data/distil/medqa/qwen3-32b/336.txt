### Capacity to Make Healthcare Decisions

The determination of **decision-making capacity** is not about whether a person is "normal" or "sane," but rather whether they can:

1. **Understand** the nature of the medical condition and the proposed intervention.
2. **Appreciate** the consequences of accepting or refusing the intervention.
3. **Reason** about the options and their implications.
4. **Express** a choice.

In this case, the patient is **not oriented to time or place**, **combative**, and **does not respond to staff questions**. These are **clinical signs of impaired decision-making capacity**, especially when due to **delirium**, which is a **reversible cause of acute confusion**. Delirium can significantly impair a patient’s ability to **understand and appreciate** medical decisions, even in someone with a baseline cognitive disorder.

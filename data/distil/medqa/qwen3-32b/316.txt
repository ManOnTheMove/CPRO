### Clinical Presentation and Key Findings

A 28-year-old man presents with:

- **Acute left knee inflammation** (warm, erythematous, tender, limited range of motion)

---

### Differential Diagnosis and Clinical Clues

This clinical picture is most consistent with **reactive arthritis (Reiter’s syndrome)**, a seronegative spondyloarthropathy. The **classic triad** of reactive arthritis includes:

1. **Arthritis** – Often asymmetric, involving large joints (here, the left knee)
2. **Urethritis or dysuria** – Suggestive of a genitourinary infection
3. **Conjunctivitis** – Bilateral in this case

These symptoms are typically **post-infectious**, commonly following **urethral (e.g., *Chlamydia trachomatis*) or gastrointestinal infections**. The patient’s sexual activity and inconsistent condom use raise suspicion for a sexually transmitted infection, particularly chlamydia, as a trigger.

---

### Laboratory and Genetic Associations

Reactive arthritis is **strongly associated with the HLA-B27** genotype. Approximately **80% of patients** with reactive arthritis are HLA-B27 positive. While the presence of HLA-B27 is not diagnostic on its own, it is a **key marker** in the context of the clinical triad and supports the diagnosis.

---

- **B. Hiking trip two months ago**: May suggest **Lyme disease**, but the timeline and absence of a classic erythema migrans rash make this unlikely. The acute presentation and triad of symptoms point more strongly to **reactive arthritis** than a tick-borne illness.

1. **Timing of Onset (2 weeks post-trauma/enucleation):**
   - This is within the typical timeframe for **sympathetic ophthalmia**, which usually occurs **1–8 weeks** after penetrating injury or surgery involving the uveal tract.

2. **Enucleation of the Right Eye:**
   - Enucleation may lead to exposure of uveal tissue, which can trigger an **autoimmune reaction** in the contralateral eye.

3. **Symptoms in the Left Eye:**
   - Redness and irritation are consistent with **inflammatory uveitis**, a hallmark of sympathetic ophthalmia.
   - Sudden, significant vision loss (20/200) is not typical of diabetic retinopathy or seasonal allergies.

  - **Less likely** without a clear unilateral cause or trauma history.

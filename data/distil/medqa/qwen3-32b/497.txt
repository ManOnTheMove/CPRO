### Understanding Alpha-Fetoprotein (AFP)

AFP is a glycoprotein synthesized primarily by the fetal liver and yolk sac. Its levels in maternal serum are measured as part of the **quad screen** or **triple screen** between 15 and 20 weeks of gestation. The test is used to screen for **neural tube defects (NTDs)** and **chromosomal abnormalities**.

Elevated AFP levels are typically associated with:

1. **Neural tube defects** (e.g., spina bifida, anencephaly)
2. **Abdominal wall defects** (e.g., omphalocele)
3. **Inaccurate gestational age** (i.e., the fetus is older than expected for the gestational age)

Conversely, **low AFP levels** are often seen in **chromosomal abnormalities** such as:

- **Trisomy 21 (Down syndrome)**
- **Trisomy 18 (Edwards syndrome)**
- **Trisomy 13 (Patau syndrome)**

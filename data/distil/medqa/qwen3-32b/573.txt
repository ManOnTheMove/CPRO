- **Acral vitiligo** and **skin hyperpigmentation** are classic signs of **autoimmune adrenal insufficiency**.
- **Low serum sodium**, **elevated potassium**, and **hypoglycemia** are hallmark electrolyte and metabolic disturbances in adrenal crisis.

### Treatment Strategy

In suspected **acute adrenal crisis**, **immediate treatment** is required without waiting for confirmatory tests. The mainstays of treatment are:

1. **Intravenous fluids (normal saline)** – to correct hypovolemia.
2. **Intravenous corticosteroids (e.g., dexamethasone)** – to replace deficient cortisol and reverse the crisis.

- **Dexamethasone** is preferred over hydrocortisone in this scenario because it has **no mineralocorticoid activity**, allowing for later titration of mineralocorticoid replacement based on blood pressure and electrolyte response.
- **Normal saline** addresses volume depletion, which is a major contributor to hypotension in adrenal insufficiency.

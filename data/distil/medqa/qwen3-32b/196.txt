### Pathophysiology of Multiple Myeloma and Amyloidosis

**Multiple myeloma** is a neoplastic disorder characterized by the clonal proliferation of plasma cells in the bone marrow. These malignant plasma cells produce excessive monoclonal immunoglobulin (M protein), often in the form of **light chains** (either kappa or lambda). These abnormal light chains can misfold and form insoluble fibrils that deposit in various organs, most commonly the **kidneys**.

When these deposits occur in tissues, it is referred to as **amyloidosis**. The specific form associated with multiple myeloma is **AL amyloidosis**, where:

- **A** stands for **Amyloid**
- **L** stands for **Light chain** (from immunoglobulins)

These deposits can infiltrate the glomeruli and tubules of the kidney, leading to **proteinuria** and **renal dysfunction**, which aligns with the clinical findings in this patient.

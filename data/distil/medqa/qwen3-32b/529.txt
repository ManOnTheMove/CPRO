### Auscultatory Features of Mitral Stenosis

In mitral stenosis, the key auscultatory findings include:

- A **low-pitched, diastolic rumbling murmur** at the apex.
- An **opening snap** that follows the second heart sound (S2).
- **Pulmonary hypertension** may lead to a **loud P2 component of S2**.
- **S1 is usually loud** due to increased velocity of mitral valve closure.

The **opening snap** is a key feature. It occurs when the **mitral valve opens** during early diastole. The **timing** of this snap relative to S2 is **inversely related to the severity** of the stenosis:

- In **mild stenosis**, the left atrial pressure is lower, and the valve opens **later** in diastole → **longer interval** between S2 and the opening snap.
- In **severe stenosis**, the left atrial pressure is elevated, and the valve opens **earlier** → **shorter interval** between S2 and the opening snap.

Thus, a **decreased time between S2 and the opening snap** indicates **more severe mitral stenosis**.

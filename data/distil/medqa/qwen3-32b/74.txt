To analyze the effect of molecule A on the enzyme kinetics of the novel drug, we must first identify the type of enzyme inhibition involved. The question states that molecule A **binds reversibly at the same active site** as the drug. This is the hallmark of **competitive inhibition**.

---

### Understanding Competitive Inhibition

In **competitive inhibition**:

- The inhibitor **competes with the substrate** (here, the drug) for the enzyme's active site.
- The presence of the inhibitor **reduces the enzyme's affinity** for the substrate, meaning a **higher substrate concentration is required** to achieve the same reaction rate.
- However, the **maximum velocity (Vmax)** of the reaction remains unchanged because, at very high substrate concentrations, the inhibitor can be outcompeted, allowing the enzyme to reach its full catalytic capacity.

---

### Effect on Enzyme Kinetic Parameters

- **Vmax (Maximum Reaction Rate)**: **Unchanged**. The enzyme is still capable of functioning at full capacity if enough substrate is available.
- **Km (Michaelis constant)**: **Increased**. The enzyme now has a **lower affinity** for the substrate, so a **higher concentration** of the drug is required to reach half of the maximum velocity.

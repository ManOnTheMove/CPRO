#### 1. **Developmental and Injury Context**
- At 17 months, children are mobile and prone to falls, but **olecranon fractures** are **uncommon** in this age group. They are typically seen in older children or adults and usually result from **high-impact trauma** or **hyperextension injuries**.
- The **pattern of bruising** (thigh, knee, elbow) is notable because bruises in **non-weight-bearing areas** in toddlers are often **incongruent with accidental trauma** and may raise suspicion for **non-accidental trauma (child abuse)**.

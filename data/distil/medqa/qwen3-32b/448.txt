### Differential Diagnosis and Pathophysiology

The clinical presentation and findings are most consistent with **cor pulmonale**, a form of **right heart failure** secondary to **pulmonary hypertension** (PH), often seen in patients with **chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)**. The underlying mechanism involves **chronic hypoxia**, **hypercapnia**, and **pulmonary vascular remodeling**, which lead to **increased pulmonary vascular resistance** and **right ventricular strain**.

The **ECG findings** (right bundle branch block and right ventricular hypertrophy) and **imaging** (enlarged pulmonary arteries) further support the presence of **pulmonary hypertension** and **right heart dysfunction**.

---

### Diagnostic Considerations

To **confirm the diagnosis** and **assess severity**, the **gold standard** test for pulmonary hypertension is **right heart catheterization**. This procedure allows for the **direct measurement** of:

- **Pulmonary artery pressure**
- **Pulmonary capillary wedge pressure (PCWP)** – to differentiate between pre-capillary and post-capillary PH
- **Cardiac output**
- **Right ventricular function**

These measurements are critical in **diagnosing and classifying pulmonary hypertension**, especially in the setting of **chronic lung disease**.

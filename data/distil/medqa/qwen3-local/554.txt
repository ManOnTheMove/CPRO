1. **Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (LVH)**:
   - This is a compensatory response to increased workload on the heart, typically due to **pressure overload** (e.g., hypertension) or **volume overload** (e.g., valvular regurgitation).
   - However, the presence of **enlargement of the left ventricular chamber** suggests **dilatation**, which is more characteristic of **volume overload** rather than pressure overload.

2. **Microscopic Findings**:
   - **Lengthening of individual muscle fibers due to duplication of sarcomeres in series** indicates **eccentric hypertrophy**.
   - Eccentric hypertrophy is a response to **increased preload** (i.e., increased end-diastolic volume), where the heart stretches and the muscle fibers lengthen to accommodate the increased volume.
   - This is distinct from **concentric hypertrophy**, which occurs due to **increased afterload** (e.g., systemic vascular resistance) and involves thickening of the muscle wall without significant chamber enlargement.

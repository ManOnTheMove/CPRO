### **Pathophysiology of Mitral Stenosis**

Mitral stenosis is most commonly caused by **rheumatic heart disease**, which is more prevalent in regions like India. In this condition:

- The **mitral valve becomes narrowed**, restricting blood flow from the left atrium to the left ventricle.
- This leads to **increased left atrial pressure** as the atrium must work harder to push blood through the narrowed valve.
- The increased pressure is transmitted to the **pulmonary vasculature**, resulting in **pulmonary hypertension**.
- Over time, this can cause **right ventricular hypertrophy** and **right heart failure**, which may be reflected in **right axis deviation** on the ECG.
- **Atrial fibrillation** is a common complication due to **left atrial enlargement** and **electrical instability**.

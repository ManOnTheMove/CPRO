Prazosin is an **alpha-1 adrenergic receptor antagonist**, which means it blocks the action of norepinephrine on alpha-1 receptors. These receptors are located in two key areas: the **smooth muscles of the urinary tract** (particularly the prostate and bladder neck) and **peripheral blood vessels**.

---

### Mechanism of Action:

1. **Relaxation of Urinary Sphincter and Bladder Neck Smooth Muscle**:
   - By blocking alpha-1 receptors in the prostate and bladder neck, prazosin causes **relaxation** of these smooth muscles.
   - This improves urinary flow and reduces symptoms like **urinary hesitancy**, which is consistent with the patient's presentation of urinary symptoms.

2. **Reduction of Peripheral Vascular Resistance**:
   - Alpha-1 receptors in the blood vessels are also blocked, leading to **vasodilation**.
   - This reduces **peripheral vascular resistance**, which in turn helps **lower blood pressure**, making prazosin effective for **hypertension**.

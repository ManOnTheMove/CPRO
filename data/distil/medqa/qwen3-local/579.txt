The RLN is a branch of the vagus nerve (cranial nerve X) and is responsible for innervating the intrinsic muscles of the larynx, which control vocal cord movement. ### Embryology of the Vagus Nerve and Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve

The vagus nerve (X) arises from the **fourth and sixth branchial arches**. Specifically:

It loops around the aortic arch (left side) or the subclavian artery (right side), and then ascends back into the larynx. During a thyroidectomy, especially a total or near-total one, the RLN can be inadvertently injured or stretched, leading to hoarseness or voice changes.

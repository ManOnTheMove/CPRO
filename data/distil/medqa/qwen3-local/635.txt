### Key Features of Malignant Hyperthermia:
- **Muscle rigidity** (especially in the trunk and limbs)
- **Rapid rise in body temperature** (often exceeding 40°C)
- **Tachycardia and hypertension**
- **Metabolic acidosis**
- **Elevated serum creatine kinase (CK)**

This condition is typically **triggered by volatile anesthetics** (e.g., isoflurane, halothane, sevoflurane) and **depolarizing neuromuscular blocking agents** (e.g., succinylcholine).

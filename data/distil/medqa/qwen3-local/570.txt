To determine which change would most effectively increase the **statistical power** of the study, we need to understand the relationship between **power**, **type II error (beta)**, and other factors.

---

### Understanding Statistical Power

**Statistical power** is defined as the probability that a statistical test will correctly reject the null hypothesis when the alternative hypothesis is true. It is mathematically expressed as:

$$
\text{Power} = 1 - \beta
$$

Where:
- **β (beta)** is the probability of a **Type II error** (failing to reject the null hypothesis when it is false).
- A **lower β** means a **higher power**.

---

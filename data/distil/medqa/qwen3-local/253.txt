### Question Review and Analysis

We are presented with a 75-year-old man who has experienced **fatigue, weight loss, and abdominal pain** over the past six months. On examination, **right upper quadrant tenderness** and **hepatomegaly** (liver palpable 2 cm below the ribs) are noted. A **liver biopsy** reveals **pleomorphic spindle cells** that **express PECAM-1 (CD31)** on their surface.

---

### Key Clues

- **Age and symptoms**: Fatigue, weight loss, and abdominal pain are **non-specific**, but when combined with **hepatomegaly**, suggest a **liver pathology**.
- **Occupational history**: The patient worked in a **plastic pipe factory**, which may involve **exposure to vinyl chloride**, a known **risk factor for angiosarcoma**.
- **Biopsy findings**:
  - **Pleomorphic spindle cells** suggest an **undifferentiated or malignant tumor**.
  - **Expression of PECAM-1 (CD31)** is a **specific marker for endothelial cells**, indicating an **angiogenic or vascular origin** of the tumor.

---

### Differential Diagnosis Consideration

#### A. **Cavernous hemangioma**
- **Benign** vascular tumor of the liver.
- Typically **asymptomatic** and **not associated with weight loss or systemic symptoms**.
- **Does not express PECAM-1** in the same way as malignant endothelial tumors.
- **Unlikely** due to the aggressive clinical presentation and PECAM-1 expression.

#### B. **Kaposi sarcoma**
- **Spindle cell tumor** associated with **HHV-8**.
- May express **CD31 (PECAM-1)**.
- Typically presents with **skin lesions** or **mucosal involvement**.
- **Uncommon in immunocompetent patients** and **not typically associated with occupational exposure** to chemicals.
- **Less likely** in this case due to the lack of skin involvement and the **strong occupational link** to angiosarcoma.

#### C. **Angiosarcoma**
- **Malignant tumor of endothelial origin**.
- **Commonly presents with hepatomegaly**, **abdominal pain**, **weight loss**, and **fatigue**.
- **Strong association** with **exposure to vinyl chloride**, which is used in **plastic manufacturing**.
- **Spindle-shaped cells** and **PECAM-1 (CD31) expression** are **characteristic** findings.
- **Most consistent** with the clinical and histological findings.

#### D. **Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC)**
- **Most common primary liver cancer**.
- Typically **associated with cirrhosis**, **hepatitis B/C**, or **alcohol use**.
- **Biopsy would show hepatocytic features**, not **spindle cells**.
- **Does not express PECAM-1**.
- **Unlikely** given the **histology and absence of cirrhosis risk factors**.

#### E. **Cholangiocarcinoma**
- **Cancer of the bile ducts**.
- **Typically presents with jaundice**, **pruritus**, and **weight loss**.
- **Biopsy would show epithelial cells**, not **spindle cells**.
- **Not associated with PECAM-1 expression**.
- **Less likely** due to **histological mismatch**.

Given the **clinical presentation**, **biopsy findings** (spindle cells with PECAM-1 expression), and **occupational history**, the most likely diagnosis is:

### Key Concepts:

- **PaO₂ (Partial Pressure of Oxygen):** Reflects the oxygen content in the alveoli and the efficiency of gas exchange in the lungs. A normal PaO₂ indicates that the lungs are functioning adequately.
- **SaO₂ (Oxygen Saturation):** Represents the percentage of hemoglobin molecules that are saturated with oxygen. This is primarily dependent on PaO₂ and the oxygen-hemoglobin dissociation curve.
- **CaO₂ (Oxygen Content):** Reflects the total amount of oxygen in the blood, which depends on:
  $$
  \text{CaO}_2 = (\text{Hb} \times 1.34 \times \text{SaO}_2) + (\text{PaO}_2 \times 0.003)
  $$
  A low hemoglobin (Hb) level reduces the total oxygen content even if PaO₂ and SaO₂ are normal.

### Understanding AFP and Its Clinical Significance

Alpha-fetoprotein (AFP) is a protein produced by the fetal liver and yolk sac. During pregnancy, AFP levels in maternal serum are measured as part of the **quad screen** or **triple screen**, which screens for chromosomal abnormalities and neural tube defects.

- **Elevated AFP** is typically associated with:
  - **Neural tube defects** (e.g., spina bifida, anencephaly) — because the open neural tube allows AFP to leak into the amniotic fluid and then into the maternal bloodstream.
  - **Inaccurate gestational age** — if the pregnancy is actually earlier than the estimated gestational age, AFP levels would be higher than expected for the given week.

- **Low AFP** is more commonly associated with:
  - **Trisomy 21 (Down syndrome)**
  - **Trisomy 18 (Edwards syndrome)**
  - **Trisomy 13 (Patau syndrome)**

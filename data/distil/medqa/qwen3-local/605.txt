### Pathophysiology and Diagnosis

In patients with **chronic kidney disease**, especially those on hemodialysis, **erythropoietin (EPO) deficiency** is a key cause of anemia. EPO is a hormone produced by the kidneys that stimulates **erythropoiesis** (RBC production) in the bone marrow. When EPO levels are low, the **bone marrow does not produce enough RBCs**, leading to **normocytic anemia** with a **low reticulocyte count**—as seen in this case.

The **normal MCV** rules out microcytic (e.g., iron deficiency) or macrocytic (e.g., vitamin B12/folate deficiency) anemias, further supporting the diagnosis of **anemia of chronic disease** or **EPO deficiency**.

### Treatment and Mechanism of Action

The **standard treatment** for anemia in CKD is **erythropoiesis-stimulating agents (ESAs)** such as **epoetin alfa**. These drugs **mimic the action of endogenous EPO** by **binding to EPO receptors on erythroid progenitor cells** in the bone marrow. This **activates signaling pathways** that promote **red blood cell production**.

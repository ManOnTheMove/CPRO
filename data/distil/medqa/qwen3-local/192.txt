The patient presents with a 2-year history of progressive memory loss, impaired short- and long-term memory, poor abstract reasoning, and social withdrawal. These symptoms are consistent with **Alzheimer's disease**, the most common cause of dementia in the elderly. 

In Alzheimer's disease, there is a significant loss of cholinergic neurons in the brain, particularly in the **basal forebrain**, which project to the hippocampus and neocortex—regions critical for memory and cognitive function. This leads to a **decrease in acetylcholine**, a neurotransmitter essential for learning and memory. The **cholinergic hypothesis** of Alzheimer's disease directly links cognitive decline to this deficiency.

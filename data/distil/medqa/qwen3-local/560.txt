### Treatment Considerations:
BPH can be managed with several classes of medications:

- **Alpha-1 blockers** (e.g., **terazosin**): These drugs **relax the smooth muscle** in the bladder neck and prostate, **improving urinary flow**. They are **fast-acting** and often used for **acute symptom relief**. However, they **do not affect PSA levels**.

- **PDE5 inhibitors** (e.g., **tadalafil**): Although primarily used for **erectile dysfunction**, they are also **approved for BPH** and can improve urinary symptoms. However, they **do not affect PSA levels**.

- **Flutamide** and **oxybutynin** are **not typically used** for BPH. Flutamide is an **anti-androgen** used in **prostate cancer**, and oxybutynin is an **anticholinergic** used for **overactive bladder**.

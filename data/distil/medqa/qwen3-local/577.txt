This autosomal dominant disorder is characterized by progressive degeneration of the basal ganglia, particularly the **caudate nucleus** and **putamen**, leading to motor, cognitive, and psychiatric symptoms. 

Key observations supporting this diagnosis include:
- **Chorea** (dance-like movements) is a classic motor feature of Huntington's.
- **Cognitive decline** (MMSE score of 22/30) and **behavioral changes** (irritability, anger) are common in the early stages.

**MRI findings** in Huntington's disease typically show **atrophy of the caudate nucleus** and **putamen**, with relative preservation of the cerebellum and cerebral cortex. This differentiates it from other conditions:
- **Cerebellar atrophy** (B) is seen in ataxias.
- **Diffuse cortical atrophy** (C) is more characteristic of Alzheimer's disease.

### Understanding the Mechanism of the Diphtheria Toxin

The **diphtheria toxin** is an **ADP-ribosyltransferase** that specifically targets **eEF-2 (elongation factor 2)**, a key component of the **translation machinery** in eukaryotic cells. This factor is essential for the **translocation** step in protein synthesis, which involves the ribosome moving along the mRNA to the next codon after each amino acid is added to the growing polypeptide chain.

By **inactivating eEF-2 through ADP-ribosylation**, the toxin **prevents ribosome translocation**. This halts the elongation phase of protein synthesis, leading to the **inhibition of cellular protein production** and ultimately causing **cell death**. This mechanism is particularly damaging in tissues like the pharynx and tonsils, where the toxin causes the formation of the characteristic **pseudomembrane**.

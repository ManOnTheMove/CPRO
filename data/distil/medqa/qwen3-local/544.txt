### Understanding the Pathophysiology of Erectile Dysfunction

Erectile function depends on the relaxation of smooth muscle in the corpora cavernosa, which is mediated by the release of nitric oxide (NO). NO activates the enzyme guanylate cyclase, leading to the production of cyclic guanosine monophosphate (cGMP). cGMP causes smooth muscle relaxation, allowing increased blood flow into the penis and the development of an erection.

The breakdown of cGMP is catalyzed by **phosphodiesterase type 5 (PDE5)**. Inhibiting this enzyme prevents the degradation of cGMP, thereby prolonging its effects and facilitating an erection.

### Pathophysiology:

In **Eisenmenger syndrome**, a **large VSD** leads to **chronic left-to-right shunting**, increasing **pulmonary blood flow** and **pulmonary vascular resistance**. Over time, **pulmonary artery hypertension** develops, eventually **reversing the shunt** to **right-to-left**, causing **cyanosis** and **systemic hypoxemia**. The **primary mechanism** driving the current presentation is therefore **pulmonary artery hypertension**, which leads to the **right-to-left shunt** and **cyanosis**.

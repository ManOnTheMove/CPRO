When determining who should provide consent for a medical procedure involving a minor, the key factor is identifying the **legal guardian** of the child. In this case, the 13-month-old child lives with his 16-year-old mother, and the 19-year-old father is not involved. 

---

### Legal Considerations

In most legal systems, a **parent is considered the legal guardian** of a minor child, regardless of the parent's age. This means that even if the mother is a minor herself, she still holds the legal authority to make medical decisions on behalf of her child **unless** there is a court order or legal intervention that revokes this authority.

The fact that the **father is not involved** is significant. While both parents typically have equal legal rights, the child's **residence and primary care** are with the mother, indicating that she is the **custodial parent**. This further supports the conclusion that the mother is the appropriate person to provide consent.

- **E: A judge** – Incorrect. A court order would only be necessary if there were a dispute over guardianship or if the mother were not a legal guardian, which is not the case here.

Given that the mother is the legal guardian and the child resides with her, the surgeon should obtain consent from the mother, even though she is a minor herself.

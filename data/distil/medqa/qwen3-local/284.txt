The 12-year-old girl presents with a 3-day history of fever, sore throat, and hematuria. ---

### Step-by-Step Analysis:

#### 1. **Clinical Presentation**
- **Fever (39.3°C)** and **sore throat** are common in both **viral** and **bacterial** infections.
- **White exudate on tonsils** and **erythematous pharynx** are more suggestive of **bacterial pharyngitis**, particularly **Streptococcus pyogenes** (Group A strep).
- **Conjunctivitis** and **otitis media** are **not typical** of strep throat, but **can occur** in viral infections.
- **Hematuria** is the **key differentiator**, as it is **not a typical feature** of acute strep pharyngitis.

---

#### 2. **Urine Dipstick Findings**
- **Blood 3+** is present, but **no protein, glucose, ketones, or leukocyte esterase**.
- **No nitrites** or **leukocyte esterase** suggests **no urinary tract infection (UTI)**.
- **No protein** rules out **post-streptococcal glomerulonephritis (PSGN)**, which typically presents with **proteinuria** and **hematuria** **1–3 weeks after strep infection**.

Thus, the hematuria is **not likely due to PSGN** or a UTI.

---

#### 3. **Differential Diagnosis by Microorganism**

- **A: *Streptococcus pyogenes***  
  - Causes **bacterial pharyngitis** with **exudative tonsillitis**, **fever**, and **lymphadenopathy**.
  - **Hematuria** is not a typical feature of acute strep infection.
  - **PSGN** is a **complication**, but it would not occur this early (within 3 days).
  - **Unlikely** to be the main cause of the **presenting symptoms** (hematuria is not explained).

- **B: Adenovirus**  
  - Causes **pharyngoconjunctival fever** (also known as "pool fever"), characterized by:
    - **Fever**
    - **Sore throat**
    - **Conjunctivitis**
    - **Otitis media**
  - **Exudative pharyngitis** can occur with adenovirus, though it is **less common** than in strep.
  - **Hematuria** is **not a typical feature**, but it may be **coincidental** or due to **other causes**.
  - The **clinical picture** fits **better with a viral etiology** than a bacterial one, especially given the **lack of protein in urine** and **no signs of UTI**.

- **C: Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV)**  
  - Causes **infectious mononucleosis**.
  - Typically presents with **severe sore throat**, **lymphadenopathy**, **hepatosplenomegaly**, and **fatigue**.
  - **Not consistent** with **conjunctivitis** or **otitis media**.

- **D: *E. coli* O157:H7**  
  - Causes **hemorrhagic colitis** and **Hemolytic Uremic Syndrome (HUS)**.
  - Presents with **diarrhea**, **abdominal pain**, and **hematuria**.
  - **No gastrointestinal symptoms** are reported, making this **unlikely**.

- **E: *Proteus mirabilis***  
  - Causes **UTI**, especially in **children**.
  - Would present with **nitrites**, **leukocyte esterase**, and **proteinuria**.
  - **Not consistent** with the given **urine dipstick** findings.

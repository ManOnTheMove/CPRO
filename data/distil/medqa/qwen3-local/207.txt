### Nephron Function and Osmolality

The **nephron** is the functional unit of the kidney, and different segments have varying capacities to reabsorb water and solutes. Key points:

- **Proximal convoluted tubule (PCT)**: Reabsorbs about 65% of the filtered water and solutes. The fluid here remains **isotonic** to plasma.
- **Descending limb of the loop of Henle**: Permeable to water, so the fluid becomes **hypertonic** as water moves out.
- **Ascending limb of the loop of Henle**: Impermeable to water but actively transports solutes (Na⁺, Cl⁻), making the fluid **hypotonic**.
- **Distal convoluted tubule (DCT)**: Reabsorbs additional solutes but is not significantly affected by ADH.
- **Collecting duct**: **Critical site** for water reabsorption. ADH increases the permeability of the collecting duct to water, allowing it to reabsorb water and produce concentrated urine. In **DI**, the collecting ducts are **impermeable to water**, resulting in **hypotonic urine**.

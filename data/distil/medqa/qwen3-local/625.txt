---

### Key Observations:
- **Normal platelet count** suggests the issue is not due to thrombocytopenia.
- **Prolonged PTT** indicates a defect in the **intrinsic** or **common coagulation pathways**.
- **Prolonged bleeding time** suggests a **platelet function defect** or **factor deficiency affecting the intrinsic pathway**.
- **Normal PT** excludes deficiencies in the **extrinsic pathway** (factors VII, X, etc.).
- **Fibrin split products negative** rules out fibrinolysis or DIC.

### Differential Diagnosis:
- **Factor VIII deficiency (Hemophilia A)**: Prolongs PTT and may cause bleeding, but **bleeding time is typically normal**.
- **Factor IX deficiency (Hemophilia B)**: Also causes prolonged PTT, but again, **bleeding time is usually normal**.
- **von Willebrand disease (VWD)**: A **common inherited bleeding disorder** that causes **prolonged PTT** (due to reduced factor VIII) and **prolonged bleeding time** (due to impaired platelet adhesion). It is also associated with **epistaxis**, which matches the patient’s history.

---

### Pharmacotherapy Considerations:
- **Prothrombin complex concentrate (PCC)** (Option A) is used for **factor II, VII, IX, or X deficiencies**, not for VWD or factor VIII deficiency.
- **Phytonadione (vitamin K)** (Option B) is used for **vitamin K deficiency**, which affects the **extrinsic pathway** and is not indicated here.
- **Rituximab** (Option C) is used for **B-cell malignancies or autoimmune conditions**, not relevant here.
- **Desmopressin (DDAVP)** (Option D) is the **first-line treatment for VWD**. It stimulates the release of **von Willebrand factor (vWF)** and **factor VIII** from endothelial cells, improving platelet adhesion and clotting.

This is a **red flag** for **right-to-left shunting** or **impaired oxygenation**, which is commonly seen in **congenital heart defects** such as **transposition of the great arteries (TGA)** or **pulmonary atresia**.

A **5/6 systolic ejection murmur** at the **left upper sternal border** is classic for **aortic stenosis** or **pulmonary stenosis**, but in a **newborn**, it may also suggest **critical congenital heart disease** with **impaired blood flow**.

#### 3. **Feeding Fussiness with Leg Curling**
This may be a **compensatory behavior** to **reduce respiratory effort** or could be related to **gastrointestinal discomfort**, but the main concern is the **cardiac findings**.

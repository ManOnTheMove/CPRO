### Metabolism of Ethanol and Its Impact on NADH/NAD+ Ratio

Ethanol is primarily metabolized in the liver by **alcohol dehydrogenase (ADH)**, which converts ethanol to **acetaldehyde**, and then by **aldehyde dehydrogenase (ALDH)** to **acetate**. Both of these reactions **reduce NAD+ to NADH**:

- **Ethanol → Acetaldehyde** (NAD+ → NADH)
- **Acetaldehyde → Acetate** (NAD+ → NADH)

This results in a **significant increase in intracellular NADH** and a **decrease in NAD+**, creating a **high NADH/NAD+ ratio**.

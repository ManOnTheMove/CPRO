### Pathophysiology of PCOS:

PCOS is a common endocrine disorder in reproductive-age women and is a leading cause of anovulatory infertility. The key features include:

- **Anovulation**: Due to disrupted hypothalamic-pituitary-ovarian axis.
- **Hyperandrogenism**: Elevated androgens (testosterone, androstenedione, DHEA-S), which can be produced by both the ovaries and the adrenal glands.
- **Insulin resistance**: Often present in obese individuals, which may exacerbate hyperandrogenism.

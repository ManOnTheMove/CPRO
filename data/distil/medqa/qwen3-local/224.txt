---

### Definition of Positive Predictive Value (PPV)

PPV is the **probability that a person actually has the disease given that they tested positive**. Mathematically, it is defined as:

$$
\text{PPV} = \frac{\text{True Positives (TP)}}{\text{True Positives (TP)} + \text{False Positives (FP)}}
$$

This means **PPV is the proportion of positive test results that are true positives**.

---

### How Disease Prevalence Affects PPV

Disease **prevalence** is the proportion of the population that has the disease. When **prevalence is low**, there are more people **without** the disease. Even a small false positive rate (due to imperfect tests) can result in a **large number of false positives**, which **reduces the PPV**. Conversely, when **prevalence is high**, there are more true positives, and the **PPV increases**.

This means:

- **Low prevalence → Low PPV**
- **High prevalence → High PPV**

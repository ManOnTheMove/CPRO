### **Understanding the Role of Renshaw Cells**

Renshaw cells are **inhibitory interneurons** located in the spinal cord. They are **postsynaptic** to motor neurons and play a critical role in **reciprocal inhibition**. When a motor neuron fires, it activates the Renshaw cell, which in turn **inhibits the same motor neuron** via the release of **inhibitory neurotransmitters** such as **GABA** or **glycine**. This process helps prevent **overexcitation** of motor neurons and maintains **motor control**.

### **Role of SNARE Proteins**

SNARE (Soluble N-ethylmaleimide-sensitive factor Attachment protein Receptor) proteins are essential for **synaptic vesicle fusion** with the presynaptic membrane. This fusion allows for the **release of neurotransmitters** into the synaptic cleft. If a protease cleaves SNARE proteins, the **release of neurotransmitters is blocked**, including those from Renshaw cells.

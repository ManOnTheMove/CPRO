### Key Concepts in Organ Rejection

After a liver transplant, the **recipient's immune system** recognizes the **donor's allogeneic MHC (Major Histocompatibility Complex) molecules** as foreign. This recognition triggers a T cell-mediated immune response, which is the hallmark of **acute rejection**. There are two main pathways of antigen presentation in this context:

1. **Direct pathway**: Donor APCs (antigen-presenting cells) present donor MHC molecules directly to recipient T cells. This is a rapid and strong response, often seen in early acute rejection.
   
2. **Indirect pathway**: Recipient APCs take up donor MHC molecules, process them into peptides, and then present these peptides on **recipient MHC molecules** to recipient T cells. This is a slower, more chronic form of rejection.

### Key Considerations:
- **IVIG Therapy and Reactions**: IVIG is a blood product containing immunoglobulins, primarily IgG, but also traces of IgA. While it is generally safe, adverse reactions can occur, especially in individuals with underlying immunodeficiencies.
- **Anaphylaxis to IVIG**: This is most commonly associated with **IgA deficiency**. Patients with IgA deficiency may have developed **anti-IgA antibodies**, which can react with the small amounts of IgA present in IVIG, triggering anaphylactic or allergic reactions.
- **Other Immunodeficiencies**:

  - **Common variable immunodeficiency** (D): Involves low IgG and IgA, but again, not specifically associated with IgA antibodies against IVIG.
  - **Hyper-IgD syndrome** (E): A periodic fever syndrome, not related to IgA or anaphylaxis.

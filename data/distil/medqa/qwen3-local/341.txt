### Key Considerations:

1. **Sickle Cell Disease (SCD) and Hip Pain**:
   - SCD is a known risk factor for **avascular necrosis (AVN)** of the femoral head, especially in children. This is due to the vaso-occlusive nature of the disease, which can lead to ischemia and subsequent bone death.
   - AVN typically presents with **hip pain, limping, and decreased range of motion**, often without acute signs of infection (e.g., fever, warmth, redness).

2. **Differential Diagnosis**:
   - **Vaso-occlusive crisis**: Possible, but this is usually more generalized and not isolated to the hip.
   - **Septic arthritis**: Less likely due to the absence of fever, warmth, or erythema.
   - **Avascular necrosis (AVN)**: The most likely diagnosis in this context, given the chronic nature of the pain, the child's age, and the presence of sickle cell disease.

---

### Evaluation and Next Step:

- **Imaging** is crucial in evaluating hip pain in SCD.
- **Radiographs (X-rays)** are often the initial imaging modality. However, **early AVN may not be visible on X-rays**.
- **MRI** is the **most sensitive and specific** imaging modality for detecting **early avascular necrosis** and is the **preferred next step** when clinical suspicion is high.

---

### Why Other Options Are Less Appropriate:

- **Arthrocentesis (A)**: Useful for ruling out septic arthritis, but the absence of fever, warmth, or swelling makes this less likely. Additionally, it is an invasive procedure and not the first-line step.
- **CT (B)**: Not typically used for evaluating AVN. CT is more for trauma or detecting calcifications.
- **Ibuprofen and Acetaminophen (C)**: May be used for pain management, but they are not the **next step in diagnosis**.
- **Radiograph (E)**: A reasonable first step, but **MRI is more appropriate** in the context of sickle cell disease and suspected AVN due to its superior sensitivity.

Given the clinical presentation and the high likelihood of avascular necrosis in a child with sickle cell disease, the most appropriate next step is to perform an **MRI** of the hip to confirm the diagnosis.

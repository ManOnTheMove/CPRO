### Key Consideration: Hepatitis D (HDV) Co-Infection

- **HDV co-infection** with HBV is more common in individuals who inject drugs.
- It is associated with **more severe clinical outcomes**, including **acute liver failure** and **faster progression to cirrhosis**.
- **HDV serology (e.g., anti-HDV IgM or IgG)** is essential to determine whether this co-infection is present.
- This information is **critical for treatment planning**, as **HDV requires different therapeutic strategies** compared to isolated HBV infection.

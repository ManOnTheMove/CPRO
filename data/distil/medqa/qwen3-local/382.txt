### Key Pathophysiology

**Rhabdomyolysis** is the breakdown of skeletal muscle, leading to the release of intracellular contents such as **myoglobin**, **creatine kinase (CK)**, **potassium**, and **phosphate** into the bloodstream. Myoglobin is **nephrotoxic** and can cause **acute tubular necrosis (ATN)**, a form of **intrinsic renal failure**.

In **intrinsic renal failure**, the **fractional excretion of sodium (FeNa)** is typically **> 2%**, as the damaged tubules are unable to reabsorb sodium effectively.

### Understanding Skewness and Its Implications

- **Positive skew (right skew)**: A distribution where the tail extends to the right, indicating that there are a few high values that pull the mean upward.
- In such a distribution:
  - The **mode** (most frequent value) is at the **leftmost** (lowest) end.
  - The **median** is in the middle.
  - The **mean** is **greater** than the median due to the influence of the few high values.
  
Thus, the correct order for a **positively-skewed** distribution is:

$$
\text{mode} < \text{median} < \text{mean}
$$

This means the **mean is the largest**, followed by the **median**, and the **mode is the smallest**.

- The **median** is the middle value when all visits are ordered.
- The **mean** is pulled higher by the few patients with many visits.

So the correct relationship is:

### Key Clinical Considerations:

- **Ectopic pregnancy** is the leading cause of **maternal mortality in the first trimester**, especially if it ruptures.
- **Hemodynamic instability** (hypotension, tachycardia, dizziness) indicates **active bleeding** and **hemorrhagic shock**, which requires **immediate intervention**.
- **Transvaginal ultrasound** is the **gold standard** for diagnosing ectopic pregnancy, especially in early gestation, and can confirm or rule out an intrauterine pregnancy.
- However, in cases of **acute hemodynamic compromise**, **immediate surgical intervention** is often necessary to **control bleeding and prevent further deterioration**.

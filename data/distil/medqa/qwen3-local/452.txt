### Key Laboratory Findings in Hemolytic Anemia

In **hemolytic anemia**, especially **immune-mediated** (e.g., autoimmune hemolytic anemia), the following are characteristic:

- **Increased indirect (unconjugated) bilirubin** due to the breakdown of red blood cells.
- **Decreased haptoglobin** because haptoglobin binds free hemoglobin released from lysed RBCs, leading to its consumption.
- **Elevated lactate dehydrogenase (LDH)** and **reticulocytosis**.
- **Normal or slightly increased direct (conjugated) bilirubin** if there is concurrent liver dysfunction, but this is not the primary driver here.

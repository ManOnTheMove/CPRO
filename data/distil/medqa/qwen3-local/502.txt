- **Diagnosis**: The presence of pseudohyphae on a KOH preparation is a **definitive sign** of *Candida* infection, and the clinical presentation aligns with **vulvovaginal candidiasis**.
- **Risk Factors for *Candida* Infection**:
  - **Diabetes mellitus**: High glucose levels in the vaginal environment promote *Candida* growth.
  - **Vaginal douching**: Disrupts the normal vaginal flora (lactobacilli), creating an environment favorable to yeast overgrowth.
  - **Antibiotic use**: Can reduce lactobacilli and allow yeast to proliferate.
  - **Immunosuppression** (e.g., HIV, chemotherapy).
  - **Pregnancy** or use of oral contraceptives.
  - **Hormonal changes**.
  - **Wearing tight, non-breathable clothing**.
  - **Poor hygiene** or **douching**.

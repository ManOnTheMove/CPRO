---

**Clinical Context and Diagnosis:**

- **Achalasia** is a primary esophageal motility disorder characterized by:
  - **Aperistalsis** (loss of peristalsis) in the distal two-thirds of the esophagus.
  - **Failure of the lower esophageal sphincter (LES) to relax** during swallowing.
  - **Hypertensive LES** (not incompetence, as seen in GERD).
  - **Progressive dysphagia**, often with regurgitation of undigested food.
  - **No mechanical obstruction** (as confirmed by EGD).

---

**Esophageal Manometry Findings in Achalasia:**

Esophageal manometry is the diagnostic test of choice for achalasia. It typically reveals:

- **Aperistalsis** in the distal two-thirds of the esophagus.
- **Inadequate or incomplete relaxation** of the lower esophageal sphincter.
- **Normal or increased amplitude** of contractions in the proximal esophagus (not always seen in all cases).

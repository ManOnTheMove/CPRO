To determine which of the options represents **sublimation**, we must first understand what sublimation is in the context of psychological defense mechanisms.

---

### **Definition of Sublimation**

Sublimation is a **mature defense mechanism** in which **unacceptable or socially undesirable impulses** (such as anger, aggression, or sexual desire) are **redirected into socially acceptable or constructive behaviors**. This process allows the individual to express their emotions in a **productive and adaptive way**.

---

### **Analyzing the Options**

- **Option A: "Channeling his anger about the situation into training for his track meet"**

  This is a clear example of **sublimation**. The boy is taking his **anger and emotional distress** (an unacceptable impulse) and **redirecting it into a socially acceptable and constructive activity**—training for a track meet. This aligns perfectly with the definition of sublimation.

- **Option B: "Instigating a physical fight with his girlfriend’s other partner"**

  This is an **aggressive response**, not a constructive or adaptive one. It reflects **displacement or direct aggression**, not sublimation.

- **Option C: "Making jokes about the situation and incorporating them into his comedy routine"**

  This is an example of **humor as a defense mechanism**, not sublimation. While it is a coping strategy, it does not involve redirecting an unacceptable impulse into a constructive behavior, but rather **displacing emotional distress through humor**.

- **Option D: "Not thinking about the upsetting situation now or in the future"**

  This is a form of **avoidance or suppression**, not sublimation. It involves **avoiding the emotional issue** rather than redirecting it into a constructive activity.

- **Option E: "Not thinking about the upsetting situation until after his track meet, then confronting and processing his feelings about it"**

  This reflects **delayed processing** or **rationalization**, not sublimation. It’s about **postponing emotional confrontation**, not redirecting emotional energy into a constructive activity.

---

### **Conclusion**

Only **Option A** involves the **rechanneling of unacceptable emotional impulses (anger)** into a **productive and socially acceptable activity (training for the track meet)**, which is the hallmark of **sublimation**.

### Management Considerations:
The **first-line treatment** for PMDD is **selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs)**. These medications are effective in managing the emotional and physical symptoms of PMDD and are often prescribed **continuously** or **intermittently** (e.g., starting 1–2 weeks before the expected onset of symptoms). SSRIs are preferred over other antidepressants (like SNRIs) due to their better tolerability and established efficacy in PMDD.

While **combined oral contraceptive therapy (COT)** may also be considered, it is typically used as an **adjunct** or **alternative** treatment, particularly in patients who are not candidates for SSRIs or who prefer hormonal therapy. CBT is also an effective non-pharmacologic intervention, but it is generally used as **complementary** to or **in conjunction with** medication, especially for more severe cases.

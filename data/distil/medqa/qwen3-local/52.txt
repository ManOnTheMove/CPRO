### Key Clinical Features and Their Implications:

- **Gower's sign**: This is a classic sign of **Duchenne muscular dystrophy (DMD)**. It occurs due to progressive weakness of the proximal lower limb muscles, forcing the child to use their hands to "climb" up their legs to stand.
  
- **Enlarged calves (pseudohypertrophy)**: This is also a hallmark of DMD. The apparent enlargement is due to fat and connective tissue replacing degenerating muscle fibers, not actual muscle growth.

- **Delayed motor milestones**: Delayed walking and frequent falls are consistent with progressive muscle weakness.
